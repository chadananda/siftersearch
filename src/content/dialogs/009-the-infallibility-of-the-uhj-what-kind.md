### What is the infallibility distinction

<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

When Bahá'ís say the Universal House of Justice is 'infallible,' what kind of infallibility do they mean? It can't mean infallible in the same way Bahá'u'lláh's revelation is infallible. Make the distinction precise.

</div>

#### Infallibility in decision-making role

<div class="jafar-turn">

The infallibility of the Universal House of Justice is distinct from the infallibility of Bahá'u'lláh's revelation. Bahá'u'lláh's words are considered the direct "Word of God," carrying an inherent and absolute infallibility as divine revelation. In contrast, the Universal House of Justice's infallibility is [linked to its role in decision-making](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Core%20Publications/tablets-of-bahaullah-revealed-after-the-kitab-i-aqdas#p51) — it is "incumbent" upon them to address new problems and provide solutions "according to the needs and requirements of the time," as noted in the *Tablets of Bahá’u’lláh*. This form of infallibility ensures that their decisions are divinely guided and protected from error, but it is not the same as the unchangeable truth of revealed scripture.

</div>

### Where is divine guidance mentioned

<div class="user-turn" id="round-2">

Okay, but where does it specifically say in the Bahá'í writings that the Universal House of Justice's decisions are divinely guided and protected from error? Can you point to the actual text for that?

</div>

#### Guidance affirmed by 'Abdu'l-Bahá

<div class="jafar-turn">

The Bahá'í writings affirm that the Universal House of Justice's decisions are divinely guided. 'Abdu'l-Bahá explains that the House of Justice does not act on its own thoughts and opinions; rather, it is guided by a higher wisdom, stating that it will make its decisions in accordance with the ["needs of the time"](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Tablet%20Translations/abdul-baha_tablet-on-the-functioning-of-the-universal-house-of-justice#p37). This suggests a level of divine protection and guidance in its deliberations and decisions.

</div>

### Bahá'u'lláh's words on infallibility

<div class="user-turn" id="round-3">

Wait, but you mentioned 'Abdu'l-Bahá's explanation there. That's still not the primary text from Bahá'u'lláh himself. What does Bahá'u'lláh's writings say about the infallibility of the Universal House of Justice? Show me where this idea comes directly from his words.

</div>

#### Bahá'u'lláh's unique infallibility

<div class="jafar-turn">

Bahá'u'lláh's writings clarify that He alone holds the ["Most Great Infallibility"](https://siftersearch.com/document/16712) — a unique station that distinguishes His role as the Manifestation of God. In the *Kitáb-i-Aqdas*, He asserts that there is ["no partner of the day-spring of the command in His great infallibility"](https://siftersearch.com/document/16712), emphasizing that this divine attribute is singularly His. While the Universal House of Justice is divinely guided, it does not share this absolute infallibility; rather, its decisions are protected by the guidance of Bahá'u'lláh's teachings.

</div>

### Direct reference to House's infallibility

<div class="user-turn" id="round-4">

Hold on, but you're still talking about Bahá'u'lláh's infallibility being unique. Did Bahá'u'lláh say anything specific about the Universal House of Justice's role or how its decisions are protected? Is there a direct reference to its infallibility in his writings?

</div>

#### Not directly in Bahá'u'lláh's texts

<div class="jafar-turn">

Bahá'u'lláh's writings in the *Kitáb-i-Aqdas* do not directly mention the infallibility of the Universal House of Justice. Instead, they emphasize that He alone holds the ["Most Great Infallibility"](https://siftersearch.com/document/16712), a unique station that distinguishes His role as the Manifestation of God. The concept of the Universal House of Justice being divinely guided is articulated more explicitly in the interpretations by 'Abdu'l-Bahá and Shoghi Effendi, rather than in Bahá'u'lláh's own texts.

</div>

### Reconciliation of infallibility claims

<div class="user-turn" id="round-5">

Alright, so it sounds like Bahá'u'lláh didn't directly state the Universal House of Justice's infallibility in his writings. Is it then more about the interpretations and roles defined later by 'Abdu'l-Bahá and Shoghi Effendi? How do Bahá'ís reconcile this with Bahá'u'lláh's unique claim to infallibility?

</div>

#### Unique claim and guided decisions

<div class="jafar-turn">

Actually, Bahá'u'lláh's writings focus on His own unique station, stating that He has ["no partner in the Most Great Infallibility"](https://siftersearch.com/document/16712), *Kitáb-i-Aqdas*. This infallibility is a distinct attribute of His role as the Manifestation of God. The understanding of the Universal House of Justice's infallibility comes from later interpretations by 'Abdu'l-Bahá and Shoghi Effendi, who describe its decisions as being divinely guided. Bahá'ís reconcile this by recognizing that while Bahá'u'lláh's infallibility is unparalleled, the Universal House of Justice operates under divine protection, ensuring its guidance aligns with the Faith's principles.

</div>
