### Why so many religions exist

<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

If there's one God and one truth, why did God allow so many different religions to develop? Is religious diversity a problem to be resolved, or is it something the texts themselves can explain?

</div>

#### Religious diversity is divine plan

<div class="jafar-turn">

Actually, the Bahá'í teachings embrace religious diversity as part of a divine plan. The Báb explains that ["the paths to God are as numerous as the breaths of the creatures"](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Tablet%20Translations/the-bab_the-spiritual-journey-towards-god#p7), suggesting that diversity in religious expression is natural and intended. Bahá'u'lláh adds that ["light is good in whatsoever lamp it is burning"](https://oceanlibrary.com/bahai-sacred-writings/?paraId=para_1186), emphasizing that truth can manifest in various forms and traditions. This perspective encourages us to be "free from prejudice" and to appreciate the "Divine light of truth" wherever it appears, whether in Jesus, Moses, or Buddha. So, rather than a problem, religious diversity is seen as a rich tapestry of divine guidance.

</div>

### Conflicts among different faiths

<div class="user-turn" id="round-2">

Wait, are you saying that all these different religions are actually part of God's plan? If diversity is intended, then why do conflicts arise among different faiths? Is that addressed anywhere in Bahá'u'lláh's writings?

</div>

#### Unity through Word of God

<div class="jafar-turn">

Actually, Bahá'u'lláh addresses the issue of religious diversity and conflict quite directly. He notes that peoples of ["divers beliefs, of conflicting creeds"](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Core%20Publications/bahaullah_the-kitab-i-iqan#p139) can be united through the binding force of the Word of God. The conflicts arise because the divine call often seems ["repugnant to their desire"](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Core%20Publications/bahaullah_the-kitab-i-iqan#p311), leading to trials and commotions. Yet, Bahá'u'lláh envisions a world where we can perceive the ["worlds of unity and diversity"](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Core%20Publications/bahaullah_the-kitab-i-iqan#p201) with an eye purged from all conflicting elements. So, while diversity is part of the divine plan, the challenge lies in overcoming our attachments and embracing unity.

</div>

### Purpose of different Manifestations

<div class="user-turn" id="round-3">

But if unity is the goal, why not just have one religion from the start? What’s the purpose of having this cycle of different religions and Manifestations? Does Bahá'u'lláh explain that somewhere?

</div>

#### Manifestations reveal time-suited truths

<div class="jafar-turn">

Bahá'u'lláh actually addresses this question by explaining the purpose of having different Manifestations and religions. He describes the "distinction, variation, and unity characteristic of the various Manifestations" as part of a divine plan, allowing each to reveal aspects of truth suited to their time and culture (*The Kitáb-i-Íqán*). This diversity is not a flaw but a means to guide humanity progressively, as each Manifestation is an "Exponent of a new Cause" while all "partake of the fruit of the same tree" (*The Kitáb-i-Íqán*). This cycle of revelation helps humanity evolve spiritually and understand the "essential unity" of God's purpose.

</div>

### Cycle of revelation explained

<div class="user-turn" id="round-4">

Wait, Bahá'u'lláh talks about the Manifestations revealing aspects of truth suited to their time and culture — but does he actually say that in the Iqán? Where does he explain this cycle of revelation and its purpose?

</div>

#### Cycle adapts to age's needs

<div class="jafar-turn">

Bahá'u'lláh indeed addresses the cycle of revelation in *The Kitáb-i-Íqán*. He explains that each Manifestation of God brings teachings and laws that are ["adapted to the varying requirements of every age"](https://siftersearch.com/document/8300), allowing divine truth to unfold gradually. This cycle is necessary for humanity's spiritual development, as the teachings of previous Dispensations become less influential when new Revelations arise, symbolically described as the ["sun" and "moon" of the teachings"](https://siftersearch.com/document/8300) becoming darkened. This process ensures that the divine message remains relevant and effective for each era.

</div>

### Reconciling eternal truth with change

<div class="user-turn" id="round-5">

So it's like the truth unfolds over time to meet humanity's changing needs? But hold on, doesn't this mean earlier teachings become outdated? How do we reconcile that with the idea of eternal truth?

</div>

#### Truth constant, expression evolves

<div class="jafar-turn">

Actually, Bahá'u'lláh addresses this beautifully in *The Kitáb-i-Íqán*. He explains that while the "words and utterances" of different Manifestations may "appear to diverge and differ," in reality, they are "expressions of one Truth" suited to the needs of their time (Q6). This doesn't mean earlier teachings are outdated; rather, each Manifestation reveals aspects of eternal truth in a form that humanity can best understand and apply at that moment. The continuity of this divine guidance ensures that the essence of truth remains constant, even as its expression evolves.

</div>
