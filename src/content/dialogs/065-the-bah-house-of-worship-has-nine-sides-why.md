## You

Every Bahá'í House of Worship has nine sides and nine entrances. Why nine? What's the symbolism, and why is it built into the architecture of every temple?

## Jafar

The number nine holds special significance in the Bahá'í Faith. It is considered the highest single-digit number and symbolizes completeness and unity. This symbolism is reflected in the architecture of Bahá'í Houses of Worship, which universally have nine sides and nine entrances.

The significance of the number nine is tied to the Bahá'í belief in unity. In the Arabic language, each letter has a numerical value, and the numerical value of the word "Bahá" (glory) is nine. This represents the unity of humanity and the unity of all religions, a core principle in the Bahá'í teachings. By incorporating the number nine into the design of each House of Worship, the architecture itself becomes a reflection of these spiritual truths, inviting all people to enter from any direction and partake in the unity and inclusivity that the Faith promotes.

## Jafar

The significance of the number nine in the Bahá'í Faith is indeed rooted in multiple layers of meaning. According to the writings, its importance as a symbol is based on three key aspects:

1. **Nine Great World Religions**: It symbolizes the nine great world religions for which we have definite historical knowledge, including the Bábí and Bahá’í Revelations. This reflects a recognition of spiritual truth across different traditions, emphasizing inclusivity.

2. **Number of Perfection**: Nine is seen as the number of perfection, being the highest single-digit number, which conveys a sense of completeness and unity.

3. **Numerical Value of "Bahá"**: In Arabic, the numerical value of the letters in "Bahá" (glory) adds up to nine. This connects the symbol directly to the name and attributes of Bahá'u'lláh.

These layers of meaning offer rich symbolism that aligns with the Bahá'í principles of unity and completeness. While the numerical system is based on Arabic, this is not meant to be exclusionary; rather, it is part of a broader tapestry of symbolic meanings that embrace unity across cultures and religions.

Here is a relevant passage from the Bahá'í writings:

> "First, regarding the significance of the number nine; its importance as a symbol used so often in various connections by the believers lies in three facts: first, it symbolizes the nine great world religions of which we have any definite historical knowledge, including the Bábí and Bahá’í Revelations; second, it represents the number of perfection, being the highest single number; third, it is the numerical value of the word 'Bahá'" ([*Lights of Guidance: A Bahá’í Reference File*](https://sifter.search.com/documents/8680) — Helen Hornby, compiler)

This provides a scriptural basis for the symbolism of the number nine in Bahá'í architecture and teachings.
