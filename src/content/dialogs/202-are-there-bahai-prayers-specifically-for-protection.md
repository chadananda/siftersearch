### Does Bahá'í faith include protection prayers?

<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

I'm going through a difficult time and people have suggested 'prayers for protection.' Does the Bahá'í corpus actually have prayers specifically for protection — from harm, from spiritual tests, from enemies? What does Bahá'u'lláh teach about how such prayer works?

</div>

#### Bahá'í writings include protection prayers.

<div class="jafar-turn">

YesYes, the Bahá'í writings do include prayers specifically for protection. Bahá'u'lláh, in his prayers, often asks God to "keep them safe from the mischief of Thine adversaries" and to ensure that His loved ones "shall not be put to grief" ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). These prayers reflect a deep trust in God's power to protect and guide through "the winds of tests" and "the tempests of trials" ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). The essence of such prayers is not only about seeking protection from harm but also about nurturing a steadfast heart that remains devoted to God's will, regardless of external circumstances.

</div>

### How does prayer for protection function?

<div class="user-turn" id="round-2">

That makes sense, but how does that kind of protection actually work? Is it about changing external circumstances, like keeping harm away, or is it more about changing the person who prays? What's Bahá'u'lláh's take on this?

</div>

#### Prayer transforms the person, not circumstances.

<div class="jafar-turn">

BahBahá'u'lláh's perspective on protection through prayer seems to focus more on transforming the person who prays rather than altering external circumstances. He speaks of how "the winds of tests are powerless" to deter those who are near to God, suggesting that inner strength and resilience are key outcomes of such prayers ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). Moreover, he emphasizes that "adversities are incapable of estranging them" from their spiritual path, indicating that the true protection lies in maintaining one's connection to the divine despite external challenges ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). This implies that while prayer might not always shield one from harm, it fortifies the spirit to endure and transcend difficulties.

</div>

### Can prayer change external situations too?

<div class="user-turn" id="round-3">

So, it sounds like the protection is more about building inner resilience and staying connected to God rather than just avoiding problems. But does Bahá'u'lláh ever suggest that prayer can actually change external situations, or is it always about changing the inner self? Can you point to something specific in the writings about that?

</div>

#### Prayer influences both inner and external realms.

<div class="jafar-turn">

ActuallyActually, Bahá'u'lláh does suggest that prayer can influence external circumstances as well as transform the inner self. In his writings, he beseeches God to "keep them safe from the mischief of Thine adversaries," indicating a protective aspect that extends beyond just inner resilience ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). Additionally, he speaks of God's "compelling power" and "might" as forces that can impact the world around us ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). So, while the inner transformation is a significant focus, there's also an acknowledgment of prayer's potential to affect external realities.

</div>

### How to balance prayer and practical action?

<div class="user-turn" id="round-4">

Wait, but isn't there a fine line between believing prayer influences external events and just hoping for divine intervention whenever life gets tough? How does Bahá'u'lláh address the balance between relying on prayer and taking practical action ourselves?

</div>

#### Prayer aligns with divine will, requires action.

<div class="jafar-turn">

BahBahá'u'lláh indeed addresses the balance between prayer and action. He emphasizes that while prayer is a powerful means of seeking divine assistance, it doesn't replace the need for practical action. Bahá'u'lláh speaks of the "compelling power" of God's might, suggesting that prayer aligns us with divine will but doesn't absolve us from taking responsibility ([*Prayers and Meditations*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). He also acknowledges that "adversities are incapable of estranging" those devoted to God's cause, implying that inner resilience and steadfastness are cultivated through both prayer and action ([*Prayers and Meditations*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). This suggests a harmony where prayer fortifies the spirit, enabling us to act with greater clarity and purpose.

</div>

### How should we pray to align with divine will?

<div class="user-turn" id="round-5">

That makes a lot of sense, finding that balance between prayer and action. But do you think there's a specific way Bahá'u'lláh suggests we pray to ensure we're aligning with divine will and not just asking for things to go our way? Like, what attitude or mindset should we have?

</div>

#### Pray with humility and openness to divine.

<div class="jafar-turn">

BahBahá'u'lláh suggests that the attitude we bring to prayer should be one of humility and openness to divine will. He emphasizes the importance of seeking to "rend asunder the veils that shut me out" from God's presence, suggesting a focus on drawing closer to the divine rather than merely asking for personal desires ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). The mindset should be one of devotion and trust, where adversities are seen as incapable of "estranging them from Thy Cause" ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). This implies that prayer is about aligning oneself with God's will and finding strength in His guidance, rather than simply seeking to change external circumstances.

</div>
