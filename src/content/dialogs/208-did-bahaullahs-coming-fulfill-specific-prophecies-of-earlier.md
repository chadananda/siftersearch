### Which prophecies does Bahá'u'lláh claim to fulfill?

<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

Bahá'ís claim Bahá'u'lláh's coming fulfilled prophecies in the Bible, the Qur'án, and the writings of the Báb. Which specific prophecies are claimed to be fulfilled, and how does the Bahá'í corpus argue this — particularly Bahá'u'lláh's own treatment of biblical prophecy in the Iqán?

</div>

#### Bahá'u'lláh engages with biblical prophecy.

<div class="jafar-turn">

BahBahá'u'lláh's *Kitáb-i-Íqán* deeply engages with biblical prophecy, emphasizing how past peoples have "yearningly awaited the advent" of divine messengers, only to deny them when they appeared ([*The Kitáb-i-Íqán*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8300)). He argues that this pattern of expectation followed by rejection is a recurring theme in religious history, as seen in the stories of Noah, Húd, and Moses, among others. Bahá'u'lláh highlights the importance of "detachment" from worldly concerns to truly recognize these divine figures and their messages ([*The Kitáb-i-Íqán*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8300)).

In the *Iqán*, Bahá'u'lláh also interprets specific prophecies, such as those concerning the "promised Beauty" stepping forth "from behind the veil of concealment" ([*The Kitáb-i-Íqán*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8300)). This is seen as a fulfillment of the prophecies regarding the return of Christ and the coming of the Mahdi in Islamic tradition. By examining these prophecies, Bahá'u'lláh invites believers to "ponder a while those holy words" and to strive for a deeper understanding of their meanings ([*The Kitáb-i-Íqán*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8300)).

</div>

#### Bahá'u'lláh interprets 'clouds' metaphorically.

<div class="jafar-turn">

BahBahá'u'lláh interprets the "coming on the clouds of heaven" from Matthew 24:30 as a metaphorical expression rather than a literal event. He explains that such imagery signifies the "advent of the Manifestations of God" who appear with divine authority and spiritual power, veiled in the "clouds of divine bounty" ([*The Kitáb-i-Íqán*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8300)). This metaphorical understanding aligns with his broader interpretation of prophetic language, where physical descriptions often symbolize profound spiritual realities. By this interpretation, Bahá'u'lláh ties such prophecies to his own claim as a Manifestation of God, suggesting that he fulfills these spiritual promises through his teachings and revelation.
