## You

Bahá'u'lláh's interpretation of the symbols of Christianity — the resurrection, the Second Coming — differs from most Christian traditions. What is the Bahá'í reading, and why does it differ?

## Jafar

Bahá'u'lláh's interpretation of Christian symbols like the resurrection and the Second Coming is deeply symbolic, viewing these events as spiritual and not literal occurrences. In the Bahá'í Faith, the resurrection of Christ is understood as the revival of His teachings and the spiritual awakening that His message brought to humanity. It's seen as a metaphor for the transformation of hearts and souls rather than a physical resurrection from the dead.

Similarly, the Second Coming is interpreted as the return of the Christ spirit, manifested in the new teachings and person of Bahá'u'lláh. This differs from traditional Christian beliefs that often anticipate a literal return of Christ to earth. Bahá'u'lláh's teachings suggest that the essence of all religious truth is progressive, and each Manifestation of God renews the spiritual message suitable for the age.

These interpretations differ because they emphasize the continuity of divine guidance and the idea that religious symbols often carry a deeper, spiritual meaning intended to inspire personal and collective transformation. The focus is on the enduring influence of spiritual truths across time, rather than on miraculous physical events.

## Jafar

The Bahá'í interpretation of Christian symbols like the resurrection and the Second Coming emphasizes their spiritual rather than literal significance. This approach is rooted in the belief that religious truths are often conveyed through metaphor and symbolism, designed to inspire inner transformation rather than to describe physical events.

Bahá'u'lláh and 'Abdu'l-Bahá interpret the resurrection of Christ as symbolic of the spiritual rebirth and renewal brought about by His teachings. In "Lights of Guidance," a compilation drawn from Bahá'í texts, it is stated that the resurrection is not physical but essentially spiritual, symbolizing the awakening of the inner reality of humanity:

> "Concerning the resurrection of Christ, he wishes to call your attention to the fact that in this as well as in practically all the so-called miraculous events recorded in the Gospel we should, as Bahá’ís, seek to find a spiritual meaning and to entirely discard the physical interpretation attached to them by many of the Christian sects." ([*Lights of Guidance: A Bahá’í Reference File*](https://siftersearch.com/passage/8680/3569) — Helen Hornby, compiler)

Regarding the Second Coming, Bahá'u'lláh's writings suggest that it refers to the return of the Christ spirit in a new Manifestation, rather than a literal return of Jesus to earth. This interpretation is consistent with the Bahá'í view of progressive revelation, where each Manifestation of God renews divine guidance for each age.

This symbolic approach doesn't aim to diminish the significance of these events but rather to highlight their transformative spiritual impact. By focusing on the spiritual meanings, Bahá'ís believe that the teachings of Christ continue to inspire and guide humanity, transcending the need for physical validation through miracles. The spiritual interpretations found within the Bahá'í writings aim to bridge the continuity between past revelations and the present, promoting an understanding of divine truth that evolves with humanity's spiritual capacity.
