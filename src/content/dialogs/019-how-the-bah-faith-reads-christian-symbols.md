### Bahá'í interpretation of Christian symbols

<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

Bahá'u'lláh's interpretation of the symbols of Christianity — the resurrection, the Second Coming — differs from most Christian traditions. What is the Bahá'í reading, and why does it differ?

</div>

#### Spiritual over physical interpretation

<div class="jafar-turn">

Bahá'u'lláh offers a unique interpretation of Christian symbols like the resurrection and the Second Coming. He suggests that the true ["Resurrection of Christ is the Resurrection of His Revelation"](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Tablet%20Translations/bahaullah_suratul-nush-chapter-of-exhortation#p38), emphasizing a spiritual rather than a physical event. This differs from traditional Christian views which often focus on physical resurrection. Bahá'u'lláh also interprets the Second Coming as a metaphorical event, where the return of Christ is seen in the coming of a new Manifestation of God, rather than a literal return. This approach reflects a broader spiritual understanding, moving away from literal interpretations that have historically been prevalent.

</div>

### Where does Bahá'u'lláh discuss these?

<div class="user-turn" id="round-2">

Wait, hold on a second. You’re saying these are spiritual interpretations, but where does Bahá'u'lláh actually talk about them? Is it in a specific text? And what about the Gospels? How do they describe these events? Might help to compare directly.

</div>

#### Suratu'l-Nus'h and Gospel comparison

<div class="jafar-turn">

Bahá'u'lláh addresses these spiritual interpretations in texts like the *Suratu'l-Nus'h*, where he explains that the ["true Resurrection of Christ is the Resurrection of His Revelation"](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Tablet%20Translations/bahaullah_suratul-nush-chapter-of-exhortation#p38). This interpretation moves away from a literal understanding, emphasizing the transformative power of Christ's teachings as they spread across the world. In contrast, the Gospels, such as the *Gospel of Luke*, often describe events like the resurrection in more literal terms, focusing on the physical aspects of Jesus' life and death. This difference highlights the varied approaches to understanding these profound events across different traditions.

</div>

### Why spiritual interpretation over physical?

<div class="user-turn" id="round-3">

But wait, in the Gospels like *Luke*, it seems pretty clear that the resurrection is described as a physical event. So why does Bahá'u'lláh interpret it so differently? What’s the reasoning behind seeing it as purely spiritual? And what about the Qur'án? Does it have anything to say about these events, or is it silent on their meanings?

</div>

#### Emphasizes transformative power

<div class="jafar-turn">

In the Gospels, such as *Luke*, the resurrection is indeed depicted as a physical event, with descriptions like ["the sun was darkened, and the veil of the temple was rent"](https://oceanlibrary.com/kjv-gospel-of-luke/?paraId=para_859) suggesting dramatic physical changes. However, Bahá'u'lláh offers a spiritual interpretation, viewing the resurrection as the ["Resurrection of His Revelation"](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Tablet%20Translations/bahaullah_surah-of-admonition-suratul-nush#p90), emphasizing the transformative power of Christ's teachings rather than a literal bodily return. This perspective aligns with his broader approach to scripture, where he notes that words are often filled with ["figurative meaning, explanation, wisdom and exposition"](https://oceanlibrary.com/commentary-on-surah-of-sun_bahaullah/?paraId=para_25).

As for the Qur'án, it acknowledges Jesus as a prophet and speaks of his miraculous birth and ascension but does not provide a detailed account of the resurrection event as described in the Gospels. The Qur'án's focus is more on the spiritual significance of Jesus's mission rather than the specifics of his death and resurrection. This allows for a broader interpretation that can encompass both spiritual and metaphorical understandings.

</div>

### Qur'án's view on crucifixion?

<div class="user-turn" id="round-4">

But doesn't the Qur'án say Jesus wasn't even crucified, let alone resurrected as Christians claim? That seems like a big difference. Can you show me where the Qur'án talks about this stuff? And for Bahá'u'lláh, is it just about downplaying the physical, or is there more to it?

</div>

#### Jesus not crucified, spiritual focus

<div class="jafar-turn">

The Qur'án indeed states that Jesus was not crucified, explaining that ["they crucified him not, but they had only his likeness"](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Tablet%20Translations/bahaullah-abdul-baha_tablets-of-bahaullah-and-abdul-baha-from-star-of-the-west#p860), found in Surah IV. This suggests a misunderstanding by those who claimed to have killed him. Bahá'u'lláh offers a deeper interpretation, emphasizing that while the ["elemental body"](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Tablet%20Translations/bahaullah-abdul-baha_tablets-of-bahaullah-and-abdul-baha-from-star-of-the-west#p860) of Christ was crucified, the "merciful reality and the heavenly existence" of Christ remain eternal. This perspective highlights a focus on spiritual truths and the enduring nature of Christ's essence, rather than merely downplaying physical events.

</div>

### Purpose of spiritual emphasis?

<div class="user-turn" id="round-5">

Okay, I see the Qur'án gives a different take on the crucifixion, focusing more on spiritual truths. But what about Bahá'u'lláh — why such a strong emphasis on the spiritual over the physical? Is it just about highlighting eternal truths, or is there a specific purpose behind interpreting it this way? And how does this relate to traditional Christian beliefs on resurrection?

</div>

#### Highlighting transformative teachings

<div class="jafar-turn">

Bahá'u'lláh emphasizes the spiritual over the physical to highlight the transformative power of Christ's teachings rather than the literal events. He describes the ["true Resurrection of Christ"](https://siftersearch.com/library/Baha'i/Tablet%20Translations/bahaullah_suratul-nush-chapter-of-exhortation#p38) as the "Resurrection of His Revelation," focusing on how the message of Christ spread and transformed hearts and societies. This perspective aligns with the idea that spiritual truths have a more profound and lasting impact than physical events. While traditional Christian beliefs emphasize the physical resurrection, Bahá'u'lláh's interpretation invites believers to see the enduring influence of Christ's teachings as the true miracle.

</div>
