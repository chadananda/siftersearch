<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

What does the Bahá'í Faith ask Christians, Hindus, Jews, Buddhists, Muslims to do — convert, deepen in their own tradition, or something else? The answer in practice seems messier than 'we honor all religions.'

</div>

<div class="jafar-turn">

The Bahá'í Faith encourages individuals from all religious backgrounds to recognize and embrace the teachings of Bahá'u'lláh, as He is believed to be the Manifestation of God for this age. This does imply a form of spiritual conversion, as Bahá'ís believe that each Manifestation of God brings teachings suited to the needs of the time. However, this doesn't mean abandoning the truths of one's previous faith. Instead, it involves seeing Bahá'u'lláh's revelation as a continuation and fulfillment of those teachings.

In practice, the Bahá'í Faith honors and respects all religions by acknowledging the divine origin of their founders and the truths they brought. Bahá'ís are encouraged to deepen their understanding of their own religious heritage while recognizing the unity and progression of all religious truth. This approach can indeed be complex, as it asks for both a continuity of belief and an acceptance of new teachings that build upon the past. It’s about seeing all faiths as part of a single, unfolding divine story.

</div>

<div class="jafar-turn">

The Bahá'í approach to doctrinal differences emphasizes the idea of progressive revelation. This concept suggests that religious teachings are revealed by God in a manner that is suited to the maturity and capacity of humanity at different times and places. As such, Bahá'ís believe that the core spiritual truths of all religions are the same, but their social teachings and laws change to meet the needs of the time.

Regarding the divinity of Christ, Bahá'ís affirm His station as a Manifestation of God, which means they recognize His divine nature and role. However, they interpret His divinity in a way that aligns with their understanding of all Manifestations as mirrors reflecting God's light rather than God incarnate. This perspective maintains respect for Christian beliefs while interpreting them through the lens of Bahá'í theology.

In Hinduism, the diverse array of deities and philosophies is seen as symbolic expressions of the one unknowable God. The rich tapestry of Hindu thought is acknowledged as a reflection of the spiritual truths revealed by different Manifestations who have appeared in the Indian subcontinent.

By viewing these differences as symbolic or contextual, Bahá'ís aim to honor the foundational doctrines of other faiths while promoting a unified vision of religious truth. This approach seeks to preserve the integrity and value of each religion's teachings, recognizing them as part of a greater, ongoing process of divine education for humanity.
