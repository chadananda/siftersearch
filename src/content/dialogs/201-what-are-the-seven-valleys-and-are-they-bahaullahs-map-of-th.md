The Seven Valleys is often called Bahá'u'lláh's mystical masterpiece. What are these seven stages — search, love, knowledge, unity, contentment, wonderment, and true poverty — and is this his actual map of the soul's journey toward God?

</div>

<div class="jafar-turn">

YesYes, Bahá'u'lláh's "The Seven Valleys" does map out the soul's journey toward God through these stages, each representing a deeper level of spiritual understanding and closeness to the Divine. The journey begins with the Valley of Search and progresses through stages like Love and Knowledge, ultimately leading to the Valley of True Poverty and Absolute Nothingness, where the soul is fully detached and united with God.

> "The stages that mark the wayfarer’s journey from the abode of dust to the heavenly homeland are said to be seven. Some have called these Seven Valleys, and others, Seven Cities. And they say that until the wayfarer taketh leave of self, and traverseth these stages, he shall never reach to the ocean of nearness and union, nor drink of the peerless wine. The first is The Valley of Search." ([*The Seven Valleys and the Four Valleys*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8241) — Bahá’u’lláh)

This progression is both a metaphorical and mystical path, guiding the seeker through inner transformation.

</div>

> "In this city the heaven of ecstasy is upraised and the world-illuming sun of yearning shineth, and the fire of love is ablaze; and when the fire of love is ablaze, it burneth to ashes the harvest of reason." ([*The Seven Valleys and the Four Valleys*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8241) — Bahá’u’lláh)

This imagery captures the transformative and consuming nature of love on the spiritual path.
