This perspective implies that while the divine message is inherently complete and transcendent, the earthly experience of suffering can serve as a catalyst that prepares humanity to receive and appreciate the revelation. It highlights how suffering can open the heart to spiritual truths and encourage a deeper connection to the divine message.

Moreover, Bahá'u'lláh's own trials can be seen as an embodiment of this principle. His suffering exemplifies the human condition and demonstrates the transformative power of faith and perseverance. It creates a bridge between the celestial and the terrestrial, making the revelation more accessible and relatable to humanity.

In this way, the suffering of the Messenger is not a prerequisite for revelation but a profound expression of its reality and an essential part of the divine plan to connect humanity with the transcendent truths of God's message.

Consultation in the Bahá'í context is a spiritual and practical process aimed at collective decision-making. It encourages participants to express their thoughts openly, listen carefully, and detach from personal opinions once they are shared, focusing instead on finding the truth and achieving consensus for the common good.

One practical example of how this unfolds is during Local Spiritual Assembly meetings, where members discuss community issues. It often requests feedback from National Spiritual Assemblies and other Bahá'í institutions, fostering a two-way communication channel. These institutions gather insights from their communities, which are then shared with the House of Justice. This ensures a wide range of voices are heard, reflecting diverse cultural, social, and economic backgrounds.
