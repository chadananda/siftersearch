### Are there Bahá'í prayers for the deceased?

<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

Are there specific Bahá'í prayers for someone who has just died, for those in mourning, or for the spiritual progress of the departed? What does Bahá'u'lláh provide for a family facing death?

</div>

#### Bahá'í prayers comfort mourners and departed.

<div class="jafar-turn">

YesYes, Bahá'u'lláh provides specific prayers for those who have passed away and for those who are mourning. These prayers are meant to offer comfort and spiritual support during times of loss.

> "I beseech Thee, O my God, by them and by the sighs which their hearts utter in their separation from Thee, to keep them safe from the mischief of Thine adversaries, and to nourish their souls with what Thou hast ordained for Thy loved ones on whom shall come no fear and who shall not be put to grief." ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301) — Bahá’u’lláh)

This prayer reflects the spiritual nourishment and protection offered to the departed and those left behind.

</div>

### Is there a specific Bahá'í funeral prayer?

#### Bahá'u'lláh mandates a congregational funeral prayer.

<div class="jafar-turn">

YesYes, Bahá'u'lláh did provide specific instructions for the Prayer for the Dead, which is a set prayer with a particular form. This prayer is unique in that it is the only obligatory prayer in the Bahá'í Faith that is recited in congregation, and it is specifically for the deceased.

> "The Prayer for the Dead is to be recited by one believer while all present stand in silence." ([*Kitáb-i-Aqdas*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301) — Bahá'u'lláh)

This prayer is typically recited at Bahá'í funerals and is an important part of the funeral rites.

</div>

#### Prayer aids deceased's soul and comforts mourners.

<div class="jafar-turn">

TheThe Prayer for the Dead in the Bahá'í Faith is indeed a congregational prayer, and its spiritual intention is to assist the soul of the deceased in its journey and to provide solace to the mourners. Bahá'u'lláh specifies that this prayer includes six specific passages and is the only congregational prayer required, highlighting its unique role in connecting both the deceased and the living to the divine.

> "In the Prayer for the Dead six specific passages have been sent down by God, the Revealer of Verses. Let one who is able to read recite that which hath been revealed to precede these passages; and as for him who is unable, God hath relieved him of this requirement. He, of a truth, is the Mighty, the Pardoner." ([*The Most Holy Book (Kitáb-i-Aqdas)*](https://siftersearch.com/document/16712) — Bahá'u'lláh)

This prayer's structure and communal nature emphasize its importance in the spiritual practices surrounding death.
