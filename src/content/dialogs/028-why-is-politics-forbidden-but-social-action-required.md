</div>

<div class="jafar-turn">

The Bahá'í Faith encourages its followers to actively engage in the betterment of society, but it draws a clear line when it comes to partisan politics. The prohibition against partisan politics is rooted in the belief that such involvement can lead to division and conflict, contrary to the Bahá'í principles of unity and harmony.

Instead of aligning with specific political parties or ideologies, Bahá'ís are encouraged to contribute to social change through non-partisan means. This includes participating in community-building activities, promoting social justice, advocating for human rights, and working in various social institutions that align with Bahá'í principles. The focus is on addressing issues and contributing to solutions that benefit the whole of humanity, rather than supporting specific political agendas that may divide people.

In essence, the boundary works by ensuring that Bahá'ís engage with social issues from a perspective of unity and universal principles, rather than through divisive political frameworks. This approach aligns with the vision of creating a more just and unified global society.
