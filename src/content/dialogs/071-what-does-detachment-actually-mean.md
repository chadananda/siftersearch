<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

The Bahá'í writings call for 'detachment' — but the same writings also call for engaged work in the world. How is detachment compatible with engagement?

</div>

<div class="jafar-turn">

This is a beautiful question that touches on a core spiritual paradox. In the Bahá'í writings, detachment doesn't mean withdrawing from the world or renouncing material things entirely. Instead, it refers to an inner state of freedom from attachment to the material aspects of life. It's about being able to engage fully in the world without being enslaved by it.

Bahá'u'lláh encourages us to engage in work and service as acts of worship, emphasizing that our actions should be infused with a spirit of service to humanity. Detachment, in this sense, allows us to work with love and dedication without being driven by selfish desires or material gain. It liberates us to focus on the higher purpose of our efforts, aligning our actions with spiritual principles and contributing to the betterment of society. 

So, detachment and engagement are not opposites; rather, they complement each other, allowing us to act in the world with purpose and clarity.

</div>

<div class="user-turn" id="round-2">

<div class="jafar-turn">

Bahá'u'lláh describes detachment as an inner state that liberates the soul to focus on divine love and truth. He writes:

> "Thou hast inquired about detachment. It is well known to thee that by detachment is intended the detachment of the soul from all else but God. That is, it consisteth in soaring up to an eternal station, wherein nothing that can be seen between heaven and earth deterreth the seeker from the Absolute Truth." ([*He who knoweth his self hath known his Lord: Commentary*](https://siftersearch.com/passage-url) — Bahá’u’lláh)

This passage emphasizes that detachment is not about physical withdrawal but about freeing the soul from attachments that might veil it from divine love.

As for practical steps to maintain this balance, Bahá'u'lláh exalts engaging in work as a form of worship:

> "It is enjoined upon every one of you to engage in some form of occupation, such as crafts, trades and the like. We have graciously exalted your engagement in such work to the rank of worship unto God, the True One." ([*Star of the West*](https://siftersearch.com/passage-url) — Bahá’u’lláh)

This view transforms work into a spiritual practice, aligning our daily activities with a higher purpose. The emphasis is on maintaining intention and consciousness of serving God and humanity, ensuring that our engagement does not slip into mere attachment to material outcomes.
