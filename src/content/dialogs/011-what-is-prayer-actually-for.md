### Purpose of prayer in Bahá'í Faith

<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

I grew up praying, but I've never been clear on what prayer is actually supposed to do. Is it to change God's mind? To change ourselves? What do the texts say about the mechanics and purpose of prayer?

</div>

#### Prayer transforms us, not God

<div class="jafar-turn">

Prayer in the Bahá'í Faith is more about transforming ourselves than changing God's mind. Bahá'u'lláh describes prayer as a means to "burn away the veils" that separate us from divine beauty and as "a light that will lead" us closer to God's presence ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). It's about aligning our will with divine will, seeking "the good of this world and the world to come" through God's grace ([*Supplications*](https://siftersearch.com/document/16289)). So, rather than altering God's plans, prayer is a way to open ourselves to His guidance and transform our hearts.

</div>

### Meaning of 'burning away the veils'

<div class="user-turn" id="round-2">

Okay, but when Bahá'u'lláh talks about prayer "burning away the veils," what does that actually mean? Are we just talking about feeling closer to God, or is it more than that?

</div>

#### Prayer removes barriers to divine

<div class="jafar-turn">

When Bahá'u'lláh talks about prayer "burning away the veils," it's more than just feeling closer to God. It's about removing the barriers that "shut me out from Thy beauty" and allowing us to be led "unto the ocean of Thy Presence" ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). This metaphor of veils suggests that prayer helps us overcome the limitations and distractions that prevent us from fully recognizing and experiencing the divine. It's a transformative process that immerses us "beneath the ocean of Thy knowledge," enabling a deeper connection with the divine essence ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)).

</div>

### Veils and human flaws or nature

<div class="user-turn" id="round-3">

So if prayer is this transformative process, does it imply we're all starting out pretty far from God? Like, are these "veils" more about our own flaws or just the nature of being human? And how does that connect with God's grace in the process?

</div>

#### Veils from flaws and human nature

<div class="jafar-turn">

The idea of "veils" in Bahá'í writings often refers to the barriers that separate us from God, which can indeed be seen as stemming from our own "forgetfulness and negligence" ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). These veils are not just about human flaws but also about the inherent nature of our existence, which requires effort to "turn their faces toward Thy most Great Sign" ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). God's grace plays a crucial role in this process, as it is through His "mercy" and "bountiful favors" that we are enabled to overcome these barriers and draw closer to Him ([*Prayers and Meditations by Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8301)). So, while we may start far from God, His grace is always there to aid us in our journey.

</div>

### Nature of existence and God's grace

<div class="user-turn" id="round-4">

Hold on — when you say the "nature of our existence," are you talking about just being human or something deeper? And how exactly does God's grace help us if we're the ones with the barriers? Like, is grace something we earn through our efforts in prayer, or is it given freely regardless of our actions?

</div>

#### Grace is freely given, not earned

<div class="jafar-turn">

The nature of our existence, as Bahá'u'lláh describes, is deeply intertwined with the divine. He speaks of "Nature in its essence" as the "embodiment of My Name, the Maker, the Creator" ([*Tablets of Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8270)). This suggests that being human is about reflecting divine attributes, not just existing. Regarding grace, Bahá'u'lláh emphasizes that it is "God’s all-pervasive grace, from which all grace doth emanate" and is not something we earn but is freely given ([*Tablets of Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8270)). Our efforts in prayer and action help us become more receptive to this grace, but they don't determine its availability.

</div>

### Effort in prayer if grace is free

<div class="user-turn" id="round-5">

Wait, so if grace is freely given and not something we earn, what's the point of putting in the effort with prayer and action? How do our efforts actually affect our relationship with God if grace is always there?

</div>

#### Effort prepares us to receive grace

<div class="jafar-turn">

The Bahá'í perspective sees grace as a constant, freely given by God, but our efforts in prayer and action are crucial for us to "cleanse the heart" and remove the barriers that prevent us from fully experiencing that grace ([*Prayers and Meditations*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8241)). It's not about earning grace, but about preparing ourselves to receive and recognize it. Our actions and prayers help us "turn away from imitation" and align more closely with divine will, allowing us to be more receptive to the "wellspring of divine treasures" already present within us ([*Prayers and Meditations*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8241)). So, our efforts are about transformation and readiness, not about earning something that's already offered.

</div>
