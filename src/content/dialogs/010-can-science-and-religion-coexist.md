### Compatibility of science and religion

<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

I've heard religious people say science and religion are compatible, but I've never seen a convincing argument from the scriptures themselves. Do the primary texts actually address this relationship — or is it all retrofitting?

</div>

#### Bahá'u'lláh addresses philosophers' beliefs

<div class="jafar-turn">

Actually, Bahá'u'lláh does address the relationship between science and religion directly. He speaks of philosophers like Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle, noting that they "acknowledged their belief in the immortal Being Who holdeth in His grasp the reins of all sciences" ([*Tablets of Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8270)). This suggests that true understanding of science and philosophy is not separate from a belief in God. Bahá'u'lláh also mentions that these philosophers were "highly versed in wisdom" and had a "profound knowledge of such sciences" ([*Tablets of Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8270)), indicating a harmony between scientific inquiry and spiritual insight. So, it's not just retrofitting; the texts themselves articulate this compatibility.

</div>

### Explicit statement on compatibility

<div class="user-turn" id="round-2">

Okay, I see that Bahá'u'lláh references philosophers who believed in God, but does he actually say anywhere that science and religion themselves are specifically compatible? Like an explicit statement about their relationship?

</div>

#### Bahá'u'lláh suggests intertwined relationship

<div class="jafar-turn">

Bahá'u'lláh indeed makes an explicit statement about the compatibility of science and religion. He emphasizes that leaders like Aristotle, who "discovered the power of gaseous matter," acknowledged their belief in "the immortal Being Who holdeth in His grasp the reins of all sciences" ([*Tablets of Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8270)). This suggests a view where scientific discovery and religious belief are not only compatible but intertwined, as both are seen as expressions of understanding the divine.

</div>

### Clear statement on science and religion

<div class="user-turn" id="round-3">

Wait, I'm still not convinced. You're quoting Bahá'u'lláh's admiration for philosophers' belief in God, but I don't see a clear statement about science and religion being explicitly compatible. Does he ever actually say they need each other or work together directly?

</div>

#### Bahá'u'lláh implies harmony

<div class="jafar-turn">

Bahá'u'lláh does indeed speak to the relationship between science and religion, suggesting that they are not only compatible but also complementary. He describes how philosophers like Aristotle, who "discovered the power of gaseous matter," acknowledged their belief in a divine being who "holdeth in His grasp the reins of all sciences" ([*Tablets of Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8270)). This implies a vision where scientific discovery and spiritual understanding are intertwined, each enhancing the other. The emphasis is on a harmonious relationship where both realms contribute to a fuller understanding of truth.

</div>

### Metaphor of two wings of a bird

<div class="user-turn" id="round-4">

Wait, that still sounds more like admiration for philosophers rather than a doctrinal statement about science and religion supporting each other. Does Bahá'u'lláh or 'Abdu'l-Bahá actually say somewhere that science and religion are two wings of a bird, or something along those lines? I feel like there's supposed to be a metaphor like that in the writings.

</div>

#### 'Abdu'l-Bahá uses the metaphor

<div class="jafar-turn">

Actually, 'Abdu'l-Bahá is the one who explicitly uses the metaphor you're thinking of. He describes science and religion as "two wings of a bird," stating that "a bird with one wing is unable to fly" ([*Selections from the Writings of ‘Abdu’l-Bahá*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8583)). This metaphor illustrates that both are essential for humanity's progress. Without the balance of both, our understanding and development would be incomplete. This idea underscores the Bahá'í view that science and religion must work together harmoniously.

</div>

### Bahá'u'lláh's groundwork for metaphor

<div class="user-turn" id="round-5">

That's the metaphor I was thinking of! So, in this case, 'Abdu'l-Bahá makes the explicit statement, not Bahá'u'lláh. Does Bahá'u'lláh lay the groundwork for this idea, or is it mostly 'Abdu'l-Bahá who explains it in this way?

</div>

#### Bahá'u'lláh emphasizes harmony

<div class="jafar-turn">

Actually, Bahá'u'lláh does lay the groundwork for the idea that science and religion are complementary. While he might not use the exact metaphor of "two wings of a bird," his writings emphasize the harmony between them. For instance, he speaks of the importance of "knowledge and wisdom" and how they should be "in harmony with divine teachings" ([*Social and Economic Development*](https://siftersearch.com/document/20862)). 'Abdu'l-Bahá then builds on this foundation, offering more explicit metaphors and explanations to illustrate the relationship between science and religion.

</div>
