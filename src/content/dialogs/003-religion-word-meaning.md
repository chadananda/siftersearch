## You

When 'Abdu'l-Bahá used the word "religion," what did he mean by it? Because the modern English word "religion" carries an enormous freight of post-Enlightenment baggage — institutional, voluntary, partitioned from civic life. I suspect 'Abdu'l-Bahá meant something quite different. Search the writings for how the word is used and tell me.

## Jafar

'Abdu'l-Bahá viewed religion as the essence of divine reality, which must be alive, dynamic, and progressive rather than static or institutional. He emphasized that:

> "Religion is the outer expression of the divine reality. Therefore, it must be living, vitalized, moving and progressive. If it be without motion and non-progressive, it is without the divine life; it is dead." ([*Star of the West*](http://bahai-library.com/shoghieffendi_faith_Bahá’u’lláh) — US Bahá’í News Service)

Thus, religion, in 'Abdu'l-Bahá's view, transcends mere institutional frameworks, embodying a continuous and evolving divine truth.

This suggests that while secular advancements are valuable, the integration of spiritual principles is necessary to achieve true and enduring progress.

The Bahá'í Faith offers several specific elements that may not be fully replicated within secular humanism, including:

1. **Divine Revelation**: Belief in ongoing divine guidance through Manifestations of God, which provides spiritual teachings and laws for contemporary needs.

2. **Global Vision of Unity**: A framework for the oneness of humanity that transcends national and cultural boundaries, rooted in spiritual principles.

3. **Purposeful Life**: A defined purpose and meaning in life based on spiritual growth and service to humanity, beyond material achievements.

4. **Community Life**: An emphasis on community building and the development of collective spiritual and social capacities through structured institutions.

These elements provide a unique spiritual and social blueprint that aims to unify mankind under shared divine principles.
