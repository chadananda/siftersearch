### Metaphysics of the 'Greatest Name'

<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

What is the metaphysics of the 'Greatest Name' (Bahá')? Is it merely a title, or does it carry ontological weight?

</div>

#### Greatest Name has ontological significance

<div class="jafar-turn">

The "Greatest Name" in the Bahá'í Faith is not merely a title but carries profound ontological significance. Shoghi Effendi clarifies that the Greatest Name refers to Bahá’u’lláh, who has appeared in ["God’s Greatest Name"](https://oceanlibrary.com/letters-from-the-guardian-to-australia-and-new-zealand_shoghi-effendi/?paraId=para_315), signifying His role as the Supreme Manifestation of God. Bahá'u'lláh himself describes how ["every letter of Whose appellation the Divine Names are treasured up"](https://oceanlibrary.com/commentary-on-surah-of-sun_bahaullah/?paraId=para_19), indicating that the name embodies divine attributes and adorns existence itself. Thus, the Greatest Name is deeply woven into the fabric of reality, reflecting the divine presence and authority.

</div>

#### Names in Christianity and Islam

<div class="jafar-turn">

In Christianity, the significance of names is profound, as seen in the Gospel of John where it states, ["In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God"](https://oceanlibrary.com/kjv-gospel-of-john/?paraId=para_8). This suggests that the divine name or Word is not just a label but embodies the divine presence itself. Similarly, in Islam, the names of God are revered, with each name representing a different attribute of the divine, akin to how Bahá'u'lláh describes the names of God as suns ["shining above the horizon"](https://oceanlibrary.com/commentary-on-surah-of-sun_bahaullah/?paraId=para_33), each illuminating the universe with divine attributes. Thus, across these traditions, names are not mere identifiers but carry a deeper, spiritual reality that connects believers to the divine essence.
