## You

The Bahá'í Faith teaches one earthly life followed by progress through spiritual worlds. What does the soul actually DO in those worlds? Are there activities, relationships, work?

## Jafar

The Bahá'í teachings suggest that the soul continues to develop and progress through various spiritual worlds after this earthly life. While the specifics of what the soul "does" in these realms aren't exhaustively detailed, the writings provide some insights.

'Abdu'l-Bahá describes the afterlife as a continuation of our journey, where our spiritual qualities and virtues are further refined and expressed. The soul is said to experience growth, learning, and increasing closeness to God, much like how we develop here on earth. There are references to relationships continuing in the spiritual worlds, as the bonds of love and connection transcend physical existence.

As for activities and work, while there isn't a clear depiction of "work" as we understand it materially, the soul is engaged in acts of worship, service, and further understanding of divine realities. The nature of these activities would be in harmony with the spiritual nature of those worlds, contributing to the soul's eternal progression.

## Jafar

The Bahá'í writings provide glimpses into the spiritual activities of the soul in the afterlife, but much remains beyond the full grasp of earthly understanding. Here's an excerpt that hints at the nature of these activities:

> "Their work is ours, the work of the Kingdom, but it is sanctified from what we call time and place. In prayer there is a mingling of station, a mingling of condition. Pray for them as they pray for you. When you do not know it they are able to make suggestions to you, if you are in difficulty and in a receptive attitude. But there is no phenomenal intercourse. That which seems like intercourse has a much deeper and more subtle explanation. It is not possible to put into human words these great matters." ([*Dwight Barstow Collection*](https://siftersearch.com/doc/11408) — Various, includes 'Abdu'l-Bahá and Shoghi Effendi)

This passage suggests that worship and service in the spiritual worlds are free from the limitations of time and space. The interaction with souls is not like physical interaction but is more subtle and profound, involving a mingling of conditions and spiritual stations. There is a form of communication or influence, as souls in the spiritual realm can guide or inspire those on earth.

Regarding relationships, the writings imply that these connections transcend physical existence, suggesting a kind of spiritual society or community. However, the exact nature of these connections and the structure of such a society aren't detailed in the same way we conceptualize earthly social structures. The focus is more on the enduring nature of love and spiritual bonds that continue to develop and deepen.

The above insights provide a glimpse into the rich spiritual life that awaits the soul after this physical existence, but much is left to be comprehended as we progress on our spiritual journey.
