### What do scriptures say about wealth

<div class="user-turn" id="round-1">

Jesus said a rich man can't enter heaven more easily than a camel through the eye of a needle. The Bahá'í writings speak differently. What do the primary scriptures of different traditions actually say about wealth — is it a spiritual danger or just a test?

</div>

#### Wealth is a barrier and test

<div class="jafar-turn">

The Bahá'í writings indeed offer a nuanced perspective on wealth, viewing it as both a potential barrier and a test. Bahá'u'lláh states that "wealth is a mighty barrier between the seeker and his desire," suggesting that it can hinder spiritual progress ([*The Hidden Words*](https://siftersearch.com/document/20809)). However, he also acknowledges that a wealthy person who is "not hindered by his riches" can illuminate others "even as the sun enlightens the earth" ([*The Hidden Words*](https://siftersearch.com/document/20809)). This implies that wealth itself isn't inherently negative, but the attitude towards it and its use are crucial. This contrasts with the more absolute warning in the Christian tradition about the difficulty for a rich man to enter heaven, highlighting the Bahá'í emphasis on detachment and the responsible use of wealth.

</div>

### Is wealth a barrier in Bahá'í writings

<div class="user-turn" id="round-2">

Wait, the Bahá'í perspective's intriguing, but where does the idea come from that wealth can be a "mighty barrier"? Is it really in the Hidden Words? And if it's about detachment, how does that compare to what Jesus said about giving up everything to follow him?

</div>

#### Yes, it's in The Hidden Words

<div class="jafar-turn">

Yes, the idea that wealth can be a "mighty barrier" is indeed found in *The Hidden Words* of Bahá'u'lláh. He writes that "wealth is a mighty barrier between the seeker and his desire, the lover and his beloved" ([*The Hidden Words*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8230)). This echoes the concept of detachment, similar to Jesus' teachings about giving up everything to follow him. Bahá'u'lláh also advises to "cleanse thyself from the defilement of riches" to truly advance spiritually ([*The Hidden Words*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8230)). Both perspectives emphasize the importance of prioritizing spiritual wealth over material possessions.

</div>

### Comparison with rich young ruler parable

<div class="user-turn" id="round-3">

Hold on, how does that compare to the parable of the rich young ruler in the Bible? There, Jesus tells him to sell everything and give to the poor. It seems like a call for action, not just an attitude shift. Do the Bahá'í writings have anything that speaks to that level of action regarding wealth?

</div>

#### Bahá'í writings urge action too

<div class="jafar-turn">

The Bahá'í writings do indeed speak to the importance of action concerning wealth, similar to the parable of the rich young ruler. Bahá'u'lláh emphasizes that "wealth is a mighty barrier" between the seeker and spiritual fulfillment, urging the rich to not be "hindered by his riches" from the eternal kingdom ([*The Hidden Words of Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8230)). He also calls on the wealthy to recognize that "the poor in your midst are My trust," encouraging them to act generously and not focus solely on their own comfort ([*The Hidden Words of Bahá’u’lláh*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8230)). This reflects a balance between inner detachment and outward action, aligning with the spirit of Jesus' call to give to the poor.

</div>

### Stewardship versus total renunciation

<div class="user-turn" id="round-4">

Wait, but "the poor in your midst are My trust" sounds more like stewardship than giving everything away. Doesn't Bahá'u'lláh also write about generosity and charity as a means to spiritual growth? How does that align or differ from the total renunciation Jesus talks about?

</div>

#### Emphasizes stewardship and generosity

<div class="jafar-turn">

Bahá'u'lláh indeed frames wealth as a "trust" to be used wisely, emphasizing stewardship over complete renunciation ([*The Hidden Words*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8230)). He advises the rich to "guard ye My trust, and be not intent only on your own ease," suggesting that wealth should be used to support the poor ([*The Hidden Words*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8230)). This aligns with the idea of generosity as a means to spiritual growth, where one is encouraged to "bestow My wealth upon My poor" to attain "treasures of imperishable glory" ([*The Hidden Words*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8230)). While Jesus speaks of total renunciation, Bahá'u'lláh focuses on the responsible use of wealth for the benefit of others.

</div>

### Bahá'í view on radical sacrifice

<div class="user-turn" id="round-5">

But isn't there a difference between stewardship and giving so completely that you let go of everything? Jesus's call seems more radical. Are there Bahá'í writings that talk about sacrifice on that level?

</div>

#### Speaks to profound sacrifice

<div class="jafar-turn">

Actually, Bahá'u'lláh does speak to a level of sacrifice that echoes the radical call of Jesus. He invites believers to "seek a martyr’s death in My path," suggesting that such profound sacrifice is deeply valued ([*The Hidden Words*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8230)). Moreover, He frames the act of shedding one's blood for spiritual truth as "greater in My sight than the creation of the universe" ([*The Hidden Words*](https://siftersearch.com/document/8230)). This highlights a spiritual commitment that goes beyond stewardship, embracing a willingness to give everything for the divine path.

</div>
