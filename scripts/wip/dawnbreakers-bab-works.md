# Dawn-Breakers Disambiguation — WORKS / TABLETS OF THE BÁB

> The document-entity cohort (type: **work**). No person-namesakes here, but heavy POLYSEMY — especially **"Bayán."** Each work carries a TIMELINE anchor = date + period + the coinciding event. Characterization is **GPB (doc 21310) VERBATIM where it exists** (supreme/canonical), else **Nader Saiedi, *Gate of the Heart* (doc 8632) — THE academic authority on the Báb's writings** + *Logos and Civilization* (7165), Balyuzi *The Báb* (466), DB main (21308), the Phelps inventory (bahai-library.com/inventory, in corpus), and the OoL best-known-works /en/ + /fa/ tables (script = confidence "likely").
> side context: the Báb's dispensation (Bábí, 1844–1850). Three-stage frame (Saiedi 8632 ¶7770444/446): **(1) interpretive** (to ~Jan 1846) · **(2) philosophical** (1846–47) · **(3) legislative** (Máh-Kú/Chihríq, the Bayán onward).

## ⚠ THE "BAYÁN" POLYSEMY — RESOLVE EACH OCCURRENCE BY SENSE (do not merge)

Shoghi Effendi's translation is the interpretive key. Five distinct senses; the dump's single bucket **"[13] (work) the Bayán" (line 1469)** silently conflates them — SPLIT on read:

1. **The Persian Bayán** — a WORK (entity §1 below). The pre-eminent doctrinal book; nine Váḥids.
2. **The Arabic Bayán** — a DISTINCT WORK (entity §2). "smaller and less weighty" (GPB).
3. **The whole Revelation of the Báb** — a CONCEPT, "the Bayán" = His entire Dispensation/Cause. GPB ¶21055527: *"'The Bayán,' the Báb… affirms, 'is, from beginning to end, the repository of all of His attributes, and the treasury of both His fire and His light.'"* and *"Suffer not the Bayán… to withhold you from that Essence of Being…"* — here "the Bayán" = the Revelation, not the book.
4. **The people / community of the Bayán** — the Bábís (a `concept`/community sense). GPB ¶21055527 verbatim: *"'O people of the Bayán!'… 'act not as the people of the Qur'án have acted…'"*; also the *"Váḥid of the Bayán (eighteen Letters of the Living and the Báb)."*
5. **Generic "bayán" = utterance / elucidation / exposition** — NOT an entity (e.g. titles like *"Bayán dar Jabr wa Tafwíd," "Fí Bayáni'l-I'tiqád"* — inventory false-positives; the Íqán's generic usage). Discard from the work cohort.

GPB's own gloss fixes the book vs. concept boundary: ¶21055519 renders the title **"Bayán (Exposition)"** for the book; the community/Revelation senses are vocative ("O people of the Bayán") or possessive ("the fruit of the Bayán").

## §1. The Persian Bayán  ★ flagship ★
- **canonical:** The Persian Bayán · **type: work**
- **TIMELINE:** **Máh-Kú, 1847–48** (the legislative stage), during the Báb's nine-month confinement in the fortress of Máh-Kú (Jabal-i-Básiṭ, "the Open Mountain"); the great bulk of His writings belongs to the Máh-Kú/Chihríq captivity (1847–1850). Coinciding event = His imprisonment in Ádhirbáyján under Ḥájí Mírzá Áqásí / the Amír-Niẓám. Saiedi 8632 ¶7770433: *"During the Máh-Kú period the Báb wrote some of His most important works, including the Persian Bayán and the Arabic Bayán."*
- **GPB VERBATIM (¶21055519):** *"Within the walls of that same fortress the **Bayán (Exposition)** — that monumental repository of the laws and precepts of the new Dispensation and the treasury enshrining most of the Báb's references and tributes to, as well as His warning regarding, 'Him Whom God will make manifest' — was revealed. **Peerless among the doctrinal works of the Founder of the Bábí Dispensation**; consisting of **nine Váḥids (Unities) of nineteen chapters each, except the last Váḥid comprising only ten chapters; not to be confounded with the smaller and less weighty Arabic Bayán, revealed during the same period**; fulfilling the Muḥammadan prophecy that 'a Youth from Baní-Háshim … will reveal a new Book and promulgate a new Law;' **wholly safeguarded from the interpolation and corruption** which has been the fate of so many of the Báb's lesser works, this Book, **of about eight thousand verses, occupying a pivotal position in Bábí literature**…"*
- **significance:** GPB superlative — **the peerless doctrinal work of the Bábí Dispensation**; the chief legislative book; the principal vessel of the Báb's tributes to "Him Whom God shall make manifest" (Bahá'u'lláh). 8 Váḥids of 19 + a 10-chapter 9th = 152 chapters; ~8,000 verses; left unfinished (completed in spirit by Bahá'u'lláh, per tradition). Saiedi ¶7770444: the Bayán inaugurates the **legislative stage**.
- **aliases:** Bayán-i-Fársí · the Persian Bayán · "the Bayán (Exposition)" (GPB's parenthetical gloss) · بيان فارسى (OoL /fa/, likely).
- **traps:** ⚠ ≠ the Arabic Bayán (§2 — explicitly "not to be confounded"). ⚠ ≠ "the Bayán" as the whole Revelation / the Bábí community (the four other senses above). ⚠ The genitive "Váḥid of the Bayán" in GPB ¶21055527 = the 19-member Primal Unity (Letters of the Living + the Báb), NOT the book's structural Váḥids — same word, different referent.

## §2. The Arabic Bayán
- **canonical:** The Arabic Bayán · **type: work**
- **TIMELINE:** **Máh-Kú, 1847–48** — "revealed during the same period" as the Persian Bayán (GPB ¶21055519); Saiedi 8632 ¶7770433 pairs the two as the major Máh-Kú works.
- **GPB VERBATIM (¶21055519):** *"…the **smaller and less weighty Arabic Bayán**, revealed during the same period."* (The Báb's only explicit GPB characterization of it — as the lesser sibling of the Persian.)
- **significance:** A compressed Arabic recension of the Bayán's structure and laws; companion to the Persian Bayán but secondary in weight per GPB.
- **aliases:** al-Bayán al-‘Arabí · Bayán-i-‘Arabí · the Arabic Bayán · بيان عربى (OoL /fa/, likely).
- **traps:** ⚠ A DISTINCT entity from the Persian Bayán — never merge; GPB pre-empts exactly this collision. ⚠ Both are books, distinct from the Revelation-/community-/generic senses of "Bayán."

## §3. Qayyúmu'l-Asmá'  ★ His FIRST revealed work ★
- **canonical:** Qayyúmu'l-Asmá' · **type: work**
- **TIMELINE — THE ANCHOR:** **the night of the Báb's Declaration, 22–23 May 1844, Shíráz** (the upper room of the House of the Báb, to Mullá Ḥusayn). His FIRST revealed work; its opening chapter "proceeded, in its entirety, in the course of that night of nights."
- **GPB VERBATIM:** ¶21055488: *"…that 'first, greatest and mightiest' of all books in the Bábí Dispensation, the celebrated **commentary on the Súrih of Joseph**, the first chapter of which, we are assured, proceeded, in its entirety, in the course of that night of nights from the pen of its divine Revealer."* ¶21055515: *"Already in Shíráz, at the earliest stage of His ministry, He had revealed what Bahá'u'lláh has characterized as 'the first, the greatest, and mightiest of all books' in the Bábí Dispensation, the celebrated commentary on the súrih of Joseph, **entitled the Qayyúmu'l-Asmá', whose fundamental purpose was to forecast what the true Joseph (Bahá'u'lláh) would, in a succeeding Dispensation, endure at the hands of one who was at once His arch-enemy and blood brother. This work, comprising above nine thousand three hundred verses, and divided into one hundred and eleven chapters, each chapter a commentary on one verse of the above-mentioned súrih**…"*
- **significance:** GPB/Bahá'u'lláh superlative — **"the first, the greatest, and mightiest of all books in the Bábí Dispensation"**; styled by the Bábís "the Qur'án of the Bábís." 111 chapters, >9,300 verses; the "true Joseph" = Bahá'u'lláh (the arch-enemy + blood brother = Mírzá Yaḥyá foreshadowed). The Báb uses **dhikr** ("the Remembrance") as His own title throughout it (Saiedi 7165). It also recorded the birth of the Báb's infant son Aḥmad.
- **aliases:** Commentary on the Súrih of Joseph · Tafsír Súrih Yúsuf · **Aḥsanu'l-Qiṣaṣ / Aḥsanu'l-Qaṣaṣ** ("the Best of Stories" — the Qur'ánic epithet of the Joseph story, Qur'án 12:3; confirmed = Qayyúmu'l-Asmá' by Saiedi 7165: *"the Báb's Commentary on the Súrah of Joseph (Aḥsanu'l-Qaṣaṣ or Qayyúmu'l-Asmá')"*) · "the Qur'án of the Bábís" · تفسير سوره يوسف (قيوم الأسماء) (OoL /fa/, likely).
- **DUMP MERGE:** unify dump **"[2] (work) Commentary on the Súrih of Joseph" (line 1598)** + **"[7] (work) Qayyúmu'l-Asmá'" (line 1494)** + **"[1] (work) Aḥsanu'l-Qiṣaṣ" (line 1710)** → ONE entity (these are three titles for the same book).
- **traps:** ⚠ "the true Joseph" = Bahá'u'lláh (interpretive), not the patriarch. ⚠ Aḥsanu'l-Qiṣaṣ is NOT a separate work — same book. ⚠ Distinct from the GPB-attested **nine commentaries on the whole of the Qur'án** revealed at Máh-Kú (¶21055518: *"revealed no less than nine commentaries on the whole of the Qur'án — commentaries whose fate, alas, is unknown, and one of which… surpassed in some respects… the Qayyúmu'l-Asmá'"*) — those are a separate, lost corpus, NOT this work.

## §4. Dalá'il-i-Sab‘ih (the Seven Proofs)
- **canonical:** Dalá'il-i-Sab‘ih (the Seven Proofs) · **type: work**
- **TIMELINE:** **Máh-Kú / Chihríq, ~1848 (the last period of His ministry)** — GPB ¶21055522 places it "during that same period" (the Ádhirbáyján captivity); GPB ¶21055602 calls its testimony "during the last days of His ministry."
- **GPB VERBATIM (¶21055522):** *"The **Dalá'il-i-Sab‘ih (Seven Proofs), the most important of the polemical works of the Báb**, was revealed during that same period. Remarkably lucid, admirable in its precision, original in conception, unanswerable in its argument, this work… is noteworthy for the blame it assigns to the 'seven powerful sovereigns ruling the world' in His day, as well as for… the responsibilities… of the Christian divines of a former age…"* (¶21055602 quotes it on the cholera/"prodigies of the Revelation.")
- **significance:** GPB superlative — **the most important of the Báb's polemical works**; the great apologetic, adducing proofs of His mission and indicting the seven sovereigns + the Christian clergy. Has both a Persian and an Arabic version (OoL /en/ note).
- **aliases:** the Seven Proofs · Dalá'il-i-Sab‘ih (Persian) · Kitáb-i-Dalá'il-i-Sab‘ih · كتاب دلائل سبعه (OoL /fa/, likely).
- **traps:** ⚠ "Seven Proofs" (Dalá'il-i-Sab‘ih) ≠ "Seven Qualifications/Directives" (**Khaṣá'il-i-Sab‘ih**, §8) — both are "Sab‘ih" (sevenfold) works but utterly different (apologetic vs. ordinances) and from different stages (legislative-late vs. first-stage). Do not merge on the shared "Sab‘ih."

## §5. Ṣaḥífih bayna'l-Ḥaramayn (Epistle Between the Two Shrines)
- **canonical:** Ṣaḥífih bayna'l-Ḥaramayn · **type: work**
- **TIMELINE:** **the pilgrimage, late 1844 / early 1845 — literally written on the road between Mecca and Medina** ("the two holy places"). Inventory (in corpus): *"written between the cities of Mecca and Medina, as indicated by the title — 'Epistle between the two holy places' — and is listed in 'Kitáb al-fihrist' (dated 21 June 1845)."* Composed in reply to questions from Mírzá Muḥíṭ-i-Kirmání during the Ḥajj journey.
- **CHARACTERIZATION (Saiedi 8632 ¶7770648):** the Báb opens it: *"Hearken unto the command of the Remnant of God… ask ye from the Remembrance… this Arabian Youth, that which ye desire regarding the truth of the twin Sacred Shrines…"* Saiedi: *"the Báb is the Gate that unites the twin Sacred Shrines, just as He was at that moment literally situated between Mecca and Medina."* (GPB does not separately characterize it.)
- **significance:** A pilgrimage-period epistle in which the Báb, physically between the two shrines, presents Himself as the Gate (báb) uniting them; treats the symbolic meanings of the triangle and square. Listed among the works stolen on the Ḥajj journey (per the Khuṭbiy-i-Jiddah).
- **aliases:** Ṣaḥífiy-i-Bayni'l-Ḥaramayn · Ṣaḥífa bayna'l-Ḥaramayn · "Kitáb bayn al-Ḥaramayn" (inventory variant) · Epistle Between the Two Shrines / Two Holy Places · صحيفه بين الحرمين (OoL /fa/, likely).
- **traps:** ⚠ "the two shrines/Ḥaramayn" = Mecca + Medina (place-pair), not a building. ⚠ Distinct from the **Ṣaḥífih-i-‘Adlíyyih** (§6) and **Ṣaḥífih-i-Ja‘faríyyih / Raḍavíyyih / Makhzúmíyyih** (long-tail Ṣaḥífih works) — many "Ṣaḥífih" titles; disambiguate by the qualifier.

## §6. Ṣaḥífih-i-‘Adlíyyih (Epistle of Justice — Root Principles)
- **canonical:** Ṣaḥífih-i-‘Adlíyyih · **type: work**
- **TIMELINE:** **late January 1846 (2nd half of the 1st month of A.H. 1262), Shíráz** — the work that **announces the inception of the SECOND (philosophical) stage** of His writings, and **His first major work written in Persian** (Saiedi 8632 ¶7770461).
- **CHARACTERIZATION (Saiedi 8632 ¶7770461):** *"This work, sometimes also called **Ṣaḥífiy-i-Uṣúl-i-‘Adlíyyih**, on the fundamental or root principles of religion, is one of the texts that announce the inception of the second stage of the Báb's writings. It was written in the second half of the first month of 1262 (late January 1846) and is **the first major work that the Báb wrote in Persian**."* (GPB does not separately characterize.)
- **significance:** Doctrinal treatise on the uṣúl (root principles of religion); landmark as the Báb's first major Persian-language revelation — a deliberate break from the Qur'ánic-Arabic norm, opening His teaching to a wider Persian audience.
- **aliases:** Ṣaḥífiy-i-‘Adlíyyih · Ṣaḥífiy-i-Uṣúl-i-‘Adlíyyih · Risáliy-i-‘Adlíyyih (OoL roster lists "Risáliy-i-‘Adlíyyih" — likely same work, treatise vs. epistle form) · Epistle of Justice · صحیفه عدلیه (OoL /fa/, likely).
- **DUMP NOTE:** likely unifies dump **"[2] (work) Risáliy-i-‘Adlíyyih" (line 1641)** with the ‘Adlíyyih epistle — flag for review (see Hard Cases). ⚠ The companion **Risáliy-i-Furú‘-i-‘Adlíyyih** (dump line 1765 = Ṣaḥífiy-i-Furú‘-i-‘Adlíyyih, Saiedi ¶7770462, on the *branches* of religion) is a SEPARATE work — root principles vs. branches.

## §7. Commentary on the Súrih of Kawthar  ★ won Vaḥíd ★
- **canonical:** Commentary on the Súrih of Kawthar (Tafsír-i-Súriy-i-Kawthar) · **type: work**
- **TIMELINE — clean anchor:** **~May 1846, Shíráz** (the final months of the Shíráz period, after the return from Mecca, ~4 months before departure for Iṣfahán). Coinciding event = **the conversion of Vaḥíd (Siyyid Yaḥyáy-i-Dárábí)**, for whom and in whose honour it was written. Saiedi 8632 ¶7770463: *"written in honour of Siyyid Yaḥyá Dárábí (Vahid) when he met the Báb in Shíráz… he attained the Báb's presence in… Jumádi'l-Avval 1262 (April 27–May 27, 1846)… this commentary must have been written around May 1846."*
- **CHARACTERIZATION (Saiedi 8632 ¶7770463/7770633):** *"This long work interprets the Qur'ánic Súrah of Abundance through various levels of meaning."* Quoted self-statement: *"The greatest achievement of the servant is to elevate all objects to their supreme station of detachment and unity."* (GPB does not separately characterize.)
- **significance:** The work that **won over Vaḥíd** — the most eminent of the Báb's learned converts — when the Sháh's envoy sent to investigate the Báb requested it; its persuasive power converted the investigator into the disciple. (Kawthar = "Abundance," Qur'án 108.)
- **aliases:** Tafsír-i-Súriy-i-Kawthar · Commentary on the Súrah of Abundance · تفسير سوره كوثر (OoL /fa/, likely).
- **DUMP:** dump **"[3] (work) Commentary on the Súrih of Kawṯhar" (line 1551).**
- **traps:** ⚠ Vaḥíd is the RECIPIENT/occasion, not the subject. ⚠ Kawthar here = the Qur'ánic súrih title; distinct from generic "Kawthar of grace" metaphors elsewhere.

## §8. Commentary on the Súrih of Va'l-‘Aṣr
- **canonical:** Commentary on the Súrih of Va'l-‘Aṣr (Tafsír-i-Súriy-i-Va'l-‘Aṣr) · **type: work**
- **TIMELINE:** **October–November 1846, Iṣfahán — written in a single night**, in honour of the **Imám-Jum‘ih of Iṣfahán** (Mír Siyyid Muḥammad, the Báb's host). Saiedi 8632 ¶7770466: *"written in one night, in October or November 1846, during the Báb's stay in Iṣfahán, in honour of the imam jum'ih… of that city."* Inventory dates it "Stage 2 (January 1846 – April 1847)," ~22,000 words.
- **CHARACTERIZATION (Saiedi 8632 ¶7770466):** *"Interpreting various parts of the short Qur'ánic surih, the Báb discusses many fundamental issues in religion including how to recognize spiritual truth, the nature of the human being, the meaning of faith, the nature of good deeds, and the preconditions of spiritual journey."* (GPB does not separately characterize.)
- **significance:** A celebrated single-night revelation that astonished the Iṣfahán divines and helped win the Imám-Jum‘ih's protection during the Báb's Iṣfahán sojourn under Manúchihr Khán. (Va'l-‘Aṣr = "By the Afternoon/Time," Qur'án 103.)
- **aliases:** Tafsír-i-Súriy-i-Va'l-‘Aṣr · Commentary on the Súrah of the Afternoon · تفسير سوره و العصر (OoL /fa/, likely).
- **DUMP:** dump **"[2] (work) Commentary on the Súrih of Va'l-‘Aṣr" (line 1615).**

## §9. Khaṣá'il-i-Sab‘ih (the Seven Qualifications / Directives)
- **canonical:** Khaṣá'il-i-Sab‘ih · **type: work**
- **TIMELINE:** **First Stage (to January 1846)** per the Phelps inventory (in corpus, BB00562) — i.e. the early period, written before/around the pilgrimage; an Arabic text of ~140 words.
- **CHARACTERIZATION (inventory, in corpus):** title *"Khaṣá'il-i Sab‘ih (Treatise of the Seven Directives)"*; opening (original Arabic): *الامر الاول من الشعائر السبعة هو حمل الدائرة المنیعة المبارکة و الثانی منها ترک الغلیان* — English: *"The first command of the seven religious practices is to carry the blessed, protective circle. The second of them is [to abstain from the water-pipe]…"* (OoL /en/ glosses it "Seven Qualifications.")
- **significance:** A short directive epistle laying down **seven ordinances/observances** for the believers (devotional and ritual practices) — among the Báb's earliest legislative gestures. Historically associated with His instruction adding the testimony of His own station to the call to prayer (per the wider literature; not stated verbatim in the in-corpus loci — see Hard Cases).
- **aliases:** Khaṣá'il-i-Sab‘ih · Treatise of the Seven Directives · the Seven Qualifications · خصائل سبعه (OoL /fa/, likely).
- **traps:** ⚠ ≠ **Dalá'il-i-Sab‘ih** (§4, the Seven *Proofs*) — shared "Sab‘ih" (sevenfold) but a wholly different work and stage. The single most likely merge-error in this cohort.

## §10. Kitáb-i-Panj-Sha'n (the Book of Five Modes) ★ His LAST major work ★
- **canonical:** Kitáb-i-Panj-Sha'n · **type: work**
- **TIMELINE — anchor:** **the first days of spring (Naw-Rúz) 1850, Chihríq — His final year, 3–4 months before His martyrdom** (9 July 1850). GPB ¶21055555: *"In the Kitáb-i-Panj-Sha'n, **one of His last works**, He had alluded to the fact that the sixth Naw-Rúz after the declaration of His mission would be the last He was destined to celebrate on earth."* Saiedi 8632 ¶7770475: *"One of the last major works of the Báb, this book was written within the first days of the spring of 1850, the final year of the Báb's life, three or four months before His martyrdom."*
- **CHARACTERIZATION (Saiedi 8632 ¶7770475):** *"Panj Sha'n consists of nineteen chapters, each dealing with one of the names of God, corresponding to the first nineteen days of the year… These names also correspond to the nineteen figures of the Bábí Primal Unity. Panj Sha'n explains many fundamental spiritual concepts such as nearness to God, light, beauty, and unity. Each chapter was written in honour of one of the Bábís, and the chapters can all be considered as independent tablets as well. **The Tablet of Hurúfát, or Tablet of Nineteen Temples, is one of these chapters.**"*
- **significance:** GPB-attested as **one of His last works**, and it contains His own veiled prophecy of His imminent martyrdom. Title = "Five Modes (Sha'n) of Revelation" — each name of God expounded through the five modes (verses, prayers, sermons/commentaries, rational discourse, Persian). Encloses the **Lawḥ-i-Hurúfát** (Tablet of the Letters / of Nineteen Temples) as one chapter.
- **aliases:** Panj Sha'n · Kitáb-i-Panj-Sha'n · the Book of the Five Modes (of Revelation) · كتاب پنچ شان (OoL /fa/, likely).
- **DUMP:** dump **"[1] (work) Kitáb-i-Panj-Sha'n" (line 1832).** ⚠ The **Lawḥ-i-Hurúfát** (dump line 1792, "Lawḥ-i-Hurúfat") is a CHAPTER of Panj-Sha'n — relate as a sub-work, do not silently absorb (MacEoin/Saiedi treat it as a nameable tablet, "Tablet of Nineteen Temples").
- **traps:** ⚠ "one of His last works" (GPB), not necessarily the single last; pairs with the Kitábu'l-Asmá' as the two works organized on the modes-of-revelation scheme (Saiedi ¶7770502).

## CROSS-CUTTING NOTES

## ⚠ HARD CASES FOR USER REVIEW

- **A. "the Bayán" (dump line 1469, sig 13) is a five-way polysemy bucket.** It MUST be split into: (1) the Persian Bayán [work], (2) the Arabic Bayán [work], (3) the Revelation of the Báb [concept], (4) the people/community of the Bayán = the Bábís [concept/community], (5) generic "utterance/exposition" [not an entity]. The single highest-value disambiguation in this cohort. Recommend: keep "the Bayán" as the WORK = Persian Bayán by default, and create separate concept entities for senses 3–4. Needs a human ruling on how the dictionary models the work/concept split.

- **B. "Commentary on the Ṣád of Ṣamad" (dump line 1524, sig 4) is NOT a work of the Báb — it is by QUDDÚS.** The commentary on the letter "Ṣád" of the word "Ṣamad" (from Súrih Tawḥíd / Súrih of Unity, Qur'án 112) is the famous treatise **Quddús** composed during the siege of Shaykh Ṭabarsí. It belongs to the WORKS-BY-OTHER-BÁBÍS cohort, not the Báb's works. ⚠ Do NOT file it under the Báb. (Relatedly, OoL's "Súriy-i-Tawḥíd / Commentary on the Súrih of Monotheism" attributed to the Báb is a DIFFERENT text — same súrih, different author; keep apart.) Flag for human confirmation of authorship attribution.

- **C. Ṣaḥífih-i-‘Adlíyyih vs. Risáliy-i-‘Adlíyyih (dump lines 1641 + the ‘Adlíyyih epistle) — merge or keep separate?** Saiedi names the root-principles work "Ṣaḥífiy-i-‘Adlíyyih (also Ṣaḥífiy-i-Uṣúl-i-‘Adlíyyih)"; OoL's roster lists "Risáliy-i-‘Adlíyyih." These are very likely the SAME work under risálih/ṣaḥífih (treatise/epistle) variant titling — but the dump also carries **Risáliy-i-Furú‘-i-‘Adlíyyih** (line 1765), which is the SEPARATE *branches* companion. Recommend: merge Ṣaḥífih-i-‘Adlíyyih ≡ Risáliy-i-‘Adlíyyih (root principles); keep Risáliy-i-Furú‘-i-‘Adlíyyih distinct. Confirm with a primary catalog.

- **D. Lawḥ-i-Hurúfát (dump line 1792): standalone tablet or chapter of Panj-Sha'n?** Saiedi: it is "one of these chapters" of the Panj-Sha'n yet also nameable as the Tablet of Nineteen Temples. Recommend modeling it as a sub-work/part-of relationship to Kitáb-i-Panj-Sha'n rather than a fully independent or a merged entity.

- **E. Tafsír-i-Nubuvvat-i-Kháṣṣih is mis-typed in the dump.** dump line 1761 tags "Nubuvvat-i-Kháṣṣih {concept}" — but Balyuzi 466 shows the Báb wrote it as a **treatise/work** for Manúchihr Khán in Iṣfahán. Recommend re-type to `work` (the doctrine "the specific prophethood of Muḥammad" is the concept; the treatise is the work — possibly TWO entities).

- **F. Khaṣá'il-i-Sab‘ih and the adhán claim.** The seven ordinances are confirmed in-corpus (Phelps inventory); the widely-cited detail that one ordinance modified the call to prayer is NOT verbatim in the authoritative in-corpus loci scanned (GPB/DB/Saiedi/Balyuzi). Capture the seven-directives core as verified; flag the adhán specific as "likely" pending a primary citation.

- **G. "Kitáb-i-Aqdas" (dump line 1759) under the Báb's works is a mis-attribution.** The Kitáb-i-Aqdas is Bahá'u'lláh's (~1873, ‘Akká) — already enriched in gpb-enrichment-works.md §9. If the dump grouped it in a Báb-works context it is either a passing reference or a NER error; do NOT file under the Báb. (The Báb's analogous book is the Bayán.)
