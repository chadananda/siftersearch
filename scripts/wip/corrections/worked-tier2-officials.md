His own end mirrored his victims': the footnotes record he fell into disgrace, *"his veins were slashed open in the village of Fín, near Káshán, as the veins of his victims had been slashed"* (40108 h1502) — the same manner as the Amír-Niẓám's death at Fín, which the Bábí martyrs of Zanján were said to have foretold.

- **DESCRIBE (beats gen-knowledge):** A **Christian colonel** — a **Russian officer** (so he identified himself; some sources, e.g. Mu‘ín, call him "Sám Khán Urus" and suggest a later conversion to Islam, which Nabíl and ‘Abdu'l-Bahá do not reflect) — **commanding the (Armenian) Bahádurán regiment of Urúmíyyih** that was ordered to execute the Báb in the barracks square of Tabríz, **noon, 9 July 1850**. Ruhe calls him an *"erstwhile admirer of Mullá Ḥusayn."* Oppressed by foreboding, **he begged the Báb's forgiveness in advance**: *"If your Cause be the Cause of Truth, enable me to free myself from the obligation to shed your blood"* — and the Báb assured him that if he were sincere, the Almighty would relieve him of his perplexity (GPB ¶124; Taherzadeh 426). His 750 men in three files of 250 each fired in turn; **the smoke cleared to reveal the Báb unharmed and gone** (the bullets had only severed the cords; the Báb was found finishing His conversation with His amanuensis Siyyid Ḥusayn — GPB ¶125, DB). **Sám Khán, stunned, ordered his regiment to march out of the barracks and swore never again to take part in any act that would harm the Báb, even at the cost of his own life** (GPB ¶127; DB ¶1578). GPB adds the sequel: within that same year **no fewer than 250 of the firing-squad that had replaced Sám Khán's regiment perished** in an earthquake/cholera visitation (GPB ¶128) — the divine vindication of the man who withdrew.

  - ≠ any Bábí "Khán" namesakes (Naẓar Khán, ‘Abbás-Qulí Khán, etc.) — "Sám Khán" is a distinct foreign officer.
