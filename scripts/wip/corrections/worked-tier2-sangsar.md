## ‼ HEADLINE FINDING — THE "18 NUMBERED MARTYRS (#46–63)" ROSTER IS **NOT IN THE CORPUS**

The task (and `db-nayriz-tabarsi.md` §6 / `dawnbreakers-tabarsi.md` line 15) frames an **"authoritative roster, DB §20.102 (#46–63)"** of 18 Sang-Sar martyrs — Siyyid Aḥmad #46 … ‘Abdu'l-‘Aẓím #63. **That numbered roster does not exist in the ingested Dawn-Breakers text, nor in its footnotes, nor in any other corpus source I could find.** Evidence:

- I READ the actual §20.102 cluster in **21308** verbatim — para_1124–1132 (content ids 21054514–21054521). The chapter narrates the Sang-Sar episode and names **only a small family group** (see roster below). There is **no numbered list**, no "Ṣafar-‘Alí", "Karbilá'í Ismá‘íl", "‘Abdu'l-‘Aẓím", "‘Alí Khán", "Muḥammad-Ibráhím" etc. anywhere in the chapter.
- Meili `paragraphs` (typo-tolerant, whole-corpus) for the distinctive late names ("Karbilá'í Ismá‘íl … ‘Abdu'l-‘Aẓím … ‘Alí-Akbar … Sang-Sar", "Ṣafar-‘Alí Muḥammad-‘Alí ‘Alí-Aṣghar martyred Ṭabarsí") returns **no Dawn-Breakers roster** — top hits are unrelated (GPB, Nayríz docs, Karbalá-of-680-CE material).
- `40108` (footnotes) `LIKE` scans for "Sang-Sar", "Ṣafar", "‘Alí-Akbar"+"‘Alí-Aṣghar" → the only Sang-related footnote (h1410) is a **Nayríz/Vaḥíd** passage (Ṣafar whose brother Shaʿbán fell), NOT the Ṭabarsí Sang-Sar martyrs.
- The source MD itself cites the roster to **"oceanlibrary permalink /link/SJcVd/"** — and per the SKILL, the `/link/<code>/` shortlinks are **EPHEMERAL copy-range links, NOT citations**. The numbered list (#46–63) is almost certainly a **reconstruction/interpolation** the MD author pasted from a copy-range or an external list, then back-labelled "§20.102" — it is **not text we hold**.

**Consequence:** I do NOT manufacture 18 light entity records to match a list the corpus cannot substantiate (that would violate "accept partial disambiguation — flag, don't force"). I record the **family group the corpus DOES name** (deeply, with kin links + the over-merge fixes), and I FLAG the 14 bare names (#50–63) as **unverifiable-from-corpus → NEEDS-USER / NEEDS-SOURCE**. If the user has the fuller Sang-Sar roster (e.g. from Malik-Khusraví *Táríkh-i-Shuhadáy-i-Amr*, or the *Bahá'í Studies Review* 14 "Chronicle of the Bábí-Bahá'í Communities in Sangsar and Shahmirzad"), those become CREATE records — but they are NOT derivable from the present corpus.

## THE SANG-SAR EPISODE AS THE CORPUS ACTUALLY TELLS IT (§20.102, READ verbatim)

Citations all doc 21308. Chapter sequence at the fall of the fort:

- **para_1126** (cid 21054515): *"three of the companions of Quddús, who were residents of Sang-Sar, were ushered into the presence of the prince. One of them was **Siyyid Aḥmad**, whose father, **Mír Muḥammad-‘Alí**, a devoted admirer of S̱hayḵh Aḥmad-i-Aḥsá'í … had departed for Karbilá … with the intention of introducing his two sons to Siyyid Káẓim … the Prophet Muḥammad one night appeared to him in a dream, bidding the Imám ‘Alí … announce … that after his death both his sons, **Siyyid Aḥmad and Mír Abu'l-Qásim**, would attain the presence of the promised Qá'im and would each suffer martyrdom in His path."* — **the dream-prophecy of the two martyr-sons.**
- **para_1127** (cid 21054516): the *other* two Sang-Sar awakeners — **Karbilá'í ‘Alí** and **Karbilá'í Abú-Muḥammad** — foretold (1264 A.H.) the coming of "a man named Siyyid ‘Alí … preceded by a Black Standard" from Khurásán. Karbilá'í Abú-Muḥammad urged his two sons, **Abu'l-Qásim and Muḥammad-‘Alí**, to arise. Both fathers died in spring of that year (did NOT reach the fort).
- **para_1128** (cid 21054517): the two sons of Karbilá'í Abú-Muḥammad are the two companions ushered in with Siyyid Aḥmad. Siyyid Aḥmad's defiant confession before the prince (cites Shaykh Muḥammad-Ḥasan-i-Najafí's refusal; the Black-Standard hadith). The prince, reluctant to kill a siyyid, spares Siyyid Aḥmad; **"His two companions, however, were immediately put to death."** Siyyid Aḥmad + his brother **Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib** delivered to Mullá Zaynu'l-‘Ábidín to be taken to Sang-Sar.
- **para_1129–1130** (cid 21054518–19): **Mírzá Muḥammad-Taqí + seven ‘ulamás of Sárí** pursue; Mírzá Muḥammad-Taqí vilifies Siyyid Aḥmad, then "together with his seven companions, drew their swords" — **Siyyid Aḥmad is cut to pieces (MARTYRED at/near Sárí).**
- **para_1326** (cid 21054542): post-script — *"The circumstances of the martyrdom of these last two companions were related by **Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib-i-Sang-Sárí**, one of those who survived that memorable siege, in a communication he addressed to Bahá'u'lláh. In it he relates … that of his two brothers, **Siyyid Aḥmad and Mír Abu'l-Qásim**, both of whom were martyred while defending the fort … 'I recalled the counsels of my father, **Mír Muḥammad-‘Alí**…'"*

So the corpus names, for the Sang-Sar cohort: **Mír Muḥammad-‘Alí** (father, Shaykhí, foreteller — predeceased) · his sons **Siyyid Aḥmad** (martyr) + **Mír Abu'l-Qásim** (martyr) + **Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib** (survivor/chronicler) · **Karbilá'í ‘Alí** & **Karbilá'í Abú-Muḥammad** (the two awakeners — predeceased, did not reach the fort) · Karbilá'í Abú-Muḥammad's two sons **Abu'l-Qásim** & **Muḥammad-‘Alí** (the two companions "immediately put to death"). Momen 11559 adds the survivor-brother **Áqá Mír Muḥammad-Riḍá Sháhmírzádí**. That is the verifiable cohort — eight named men plus the third survivor-brother — NOT eighteen.
