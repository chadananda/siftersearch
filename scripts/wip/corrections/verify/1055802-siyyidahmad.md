## DESCRIBE
- **Cluster A (Siyyid Aḥmad-i-Sang-Sarí):** A son of the learned Mír Muḥammad-‘Alí of Sang-Sar and one of three Sang-Sar companions of Quddús taken before the Mázindarán prince after Ṭabarsí; spared execution at the fort only to be murdered en route to Sárí by Mírzá Muḥammad-Taqí, dying with the name of the Qá'im on his lips. His brother Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib survived to transmit the family's martyrdom account to Bahá'u'lláh. [Bábí]
- **Cluster B (Siyyid Aḥmad son of Vaḥíd):** A boy-son of Siyyid Yaḥyáy-i-Dárábí (Vaḥíd) who rode out from Yazd with his father and brother Siyyid Mihdí toward the Nayríz upheaval; unlike the Sang-Sar martyr he was of the Dárábí line and the sources have Vaḥíd's young sons removed to safety rather than killed. [Bábí]
- **Cluster C (Siyyid Aḥmad, father of Siyyid Ḥusayn "the beloved"):** A purely genealogical anchor in the Táríkh-i-Jadíd's Ṭabarsí account, named to place the child-martyr Áqá Siyyid Ḥusayn "the beloved" (slain aged ten) and Áqá Mírzá Muḥammad-‘Alí (maimed by a bullet) in their family; not to be confused with Siyyid Ḥusayn-i-Yazdí the Báb's amanuensis. [Bábí]
- **Cluster D (Siyyid Aḥmad-i-Afnán):** A merchant of the Báb's kindred stationed at Constantinople a generation later, moved by Bahá'u'lláh to ‘Akká and ‘Ishqábád and slandered by the Azalís in a fabricated theft — wholly separate from the 1848–49 Bábí martyrs and already correctly held as entity 1154734. [Bahá'í]
