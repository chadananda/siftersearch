## DESCRIBE (proposed)
Mullá Muḥammad-i-Mámáqání — leading Shaykhí cleric (mujtahid) of Tabríz, head of the Mámáqání Shaykhí line of Ádhirbáyján. Presided over and was the rudest interrogator at the Báb's examination in Tabríz (1848) before the Valí-'Ahd (crown prince Náṣiri'd-Dín Mírzá), and was among the Tabríz clergy who signed the Báb's death warrant / fatwá of execution (1850). Dawn-Breakers brands him "that one-eyed white-bearded renegade."
