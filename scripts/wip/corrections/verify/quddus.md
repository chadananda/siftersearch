## DESCRIBE (proposed canonical description for 1219859)
Quddús (Mullá Muḥammad-‘Alíy-i-Bárfurúshí, d. 1849). The eighteenth and last Letter of the Living and the most eminent of the Báb's disciples; a descendant of Imám Ḥasan through his mother. Accompanied the Báb on pilgrimage to Mecca; a leading figure at the Conference of Badasht (one of the three companions, with Ṭáhirih and Bahá'u'lláh); co-leader with Mullá Ḥusayn of the defense at the fort of Shaykh Ṭabarsí in Mázandarán. Titled the "Last Point" and "Last Name of God" in the Bábí Dispensation. Martyred at Bárfurúsh in 1849.
