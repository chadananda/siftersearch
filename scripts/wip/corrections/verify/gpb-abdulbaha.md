## DESCRIBE (614731)
'Abbás Effendi, titled 'Abdu'l-Bahá ("Servant of Bahá"), 1844–1921. Eldest son of Bahá'u'lláh; appointed in the Kitáb-i-'Ahd as his successor and sole authorized interpreter of his writings. Known as the Most Great Branch (Ghusn-i-A'ẓam), the Master, and the Center of the Covenant (Markaz-i-Mítháq). Led the Bahá'í Faith 1892–1921, undertook the Western journeys (1911–1913), and was the central figure of the second period of Bahá'í history covered in *God Passes By* and a recurring figure in *The Dawn-Breakers*.
