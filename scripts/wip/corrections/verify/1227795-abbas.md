## DESCRIBE
The label "‘Abbás" is one of the most over-loaded given names in the Bahá'í corpus precisely because it is ‘Abdu'l-Bahá's birth name; the disambiguation that beats general knowledge is that *four* of these men were near-contemporaries inside the same narrative frame (father Mírzá Buzurg, son ‘Abdu'l-Bahá, the Ṭabarsí enemy-general Láríjání, and the Síyáh-Chál informer-servant of Ḥájí Sulaymán Khán) yet are entirely distinct persons. [tagged: Bábí/Bahá'í history] The tree-climbing Láríjání who killed Mullá Ḥusayn is routinely conflated with Nayríz-campaign commanders and must stay firewalled. [tagged: firewall] Sháh ‘Abbás (Safavid, 16th c.) and the Qájár crown-prince ‘Abbás Mírzá belong to entirely different centuries/dynasties and only collide here through the bare-name magnet. [tagged: namesake]
