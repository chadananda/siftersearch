## DESCRIBE (per surviving node)
- **Ásíyih Khánum / Navváb** [tag: person, Bahá'í, 19thC]: Daughter of the nobleman Mírzá Ismá'íl-i-Vazír; married Bahá'u'lláh in Ṭihrán 1835; surnamed Navváb and designated "the Most Exalted Leaf." Bahá'u'lláh's tablet names her "Leaf that hath sprung from My Tree" and His "perpetual consort in all the worlds of God"; 'Abdu'l-Bahá applied Isaiah 54 to her. Mother of 'Abdu'l-Bahá, the Greatest Holy Leaf, and the Purest Branch; d. 1886; her remains were reinterred on Mount Carmel by Shoghi Effendi.
- **Navváb-i-Raḍaví** [tag: person, opponent, Qájár]: A leading Yazd notable, the most prominent of Vaḥíd's adversaries (1850), who provoked Vaḥíd at the Naw-Rúz reception and incited the populace to besiege his house.
- **Navváb Ḥamzih Mírzá** [tag: person, Qájár prince]: Royal-blood governor of Áḏhirbáyján, noted for kind-heartedness; ordered (under deception by the Amír-Niẓám) to summon the Báb to Tabríz, treating Him with consideration.
