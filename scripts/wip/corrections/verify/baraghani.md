## DESCRIBE (suggested entity description)
Powerful anti-Shaykhí / anti-Bábí mujtahid of Qazvín; first cleric to pronounce takfír (c.1822) against Shaykh Aḥmad al-Aḥsá'í, becoming the foremost opponent of Shaykhism in Iran. Paternal uncle AND father-in-law of Ṭáhirih (her husband was his son Mullá Muḥammad). Assassinated in his own mosque (Masjid-i-Jámiʿ/Jāmeʿ-e Ṣaḡīr) in Qazvín, 15 Dhu'l-Qaʿda 1263 / 25 Oct 1847, by Bábí hand(s); a Bábí, **Mírzá ʿAbdu'lláh** (per Nabíl, the actual assassin), was implicated. Known in Shaykhí reckoning as the **"Third Martyr" (Shahíd-i-Thálith / Šahīd-e Ṯāleṯ)**. His murder triggered the Qazvín persecution of the Bábís, including the confinement of Ṭáhirih (his heirs sought vengeance against her).
