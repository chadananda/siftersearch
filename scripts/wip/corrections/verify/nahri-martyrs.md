## DESCRIBE (suggested descriptions for keepers)
- **King of Martyrs (1220150)**: Mírzá Muḥammad-Ḥasan-i-Nahrí of Iṣfahán (younger of the two brothers), titled Sulṭánu'sh-Shuhadá ("King of Martyrs") by Bahá'u'lláh. With his elder brother, one of the foremost of Bahá'u'lláh's apostles ("twin shining lights," Núrayn-i-Nayyirayn). Celebrated merchant noted for generosity, trustworthiness, and piety. Beheaded at Iṣfahán on 17 March 1879 by order of Ẓillu's-Sulṭán, instigated by the Imám-Jum'ih Mír Muḥammad-Ḥusayn and Shaykh Muḥammad-Báqir ("the Wolf"). Of the Nahrí family (kin of the Afnán). His son Mírzá Jalál married Rúḥá Khánum, daughter of 'Abdu'l-Bahá (family name Shahíd).
- **Beloved of Martyrs (1220151)**: Mírzá Muḥammad-Ḥusayn-i-Nahrí of Iṣfahán (the ELDER brother), titled Maḥbúbu'sh-Shuhadá ("Beloved of Martyrs"). Martyred with his brother on 17 March 1879. Of the Nahrí family.
- **Family / Covenant links** (both): Father Mírzá Ibrahim, the Mu'tamid (RB v2 7516992). Their uncle Mírzá Hádí Nahrí was the father of Munírih Khánum (born Fáṭimih), who was raised in Iṣfahán under the brothers' care and became the wife of 'Abdu'l-Bahá (RB v2 6007164, 6007169; v2 fn 7517018). Thus the Nahrí martyr-house is bound by marriage into 'Abdu'l-Bahá's family.
