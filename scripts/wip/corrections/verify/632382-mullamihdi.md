## DESCRIBE (per keeper, beats general knowledge)
- **Khu'í (Cluster B):** Not the Letter of the Living from Khúy (that is Mullá Maḥmúd); rather a household tutor to Bahá'u'lláh's children and the trusted envoy Mullá Ḥusayn sent to wrest Quddús from the mujtahid of Sárí, after which he fell at Ṭabarsí. [TAG: bahai-history, tabarsi]
- **Kandí (Cluster C):** A Kand villager who abandoned wife and infant son for the fort, refused the prince's offer of safe exit alongside his brother Mullá Báqir, and stood the wall "fierce as a lion." [TAG: bahai-history, tabarsi]
- **Naraqí (Cluster A):** A Qájár-era Shí'í mujtahid whose *Muḥriqu'l-Qulúb* on Imám Ḥusayn's martyrdom was read to the imprisoned Báb at Máh-Kú; great-grandfather of the recipient of the Tablet of All Food. [TAG: islamic-history, bab]
- **'Aṭrí (Cluster D):** A Yazdí pilgrim of the 'Akká years, honoured with a Ziyárat-Námih, remembered chiefly as the father of the Apostle and martyr Varqá. [TAG: bahai-history, akka]
