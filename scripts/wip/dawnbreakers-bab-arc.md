## CANONICAL VERBATIM (loci fixed)

**Seven Martyrs of Ṭihrán (GPB 21310, four months before the Báb's martyrdom):** "It originated in the orders and was perpetrated under the very eyes of the irate and murderous **Amír-Niẓám**, supported by **Maḥmúd Ḵhán-i-Kalantar**, and aided by a certain **Ḥusayn, one of the ‘ulamás of Káshán**. The heroes of that tragedy were the **Seven Martyrs of Ṭihrán**, who represented the more important classes among their countrymen, and who deliberately refused to purchase life by that mere lip-denial which, under the name of **taqíyyih**…" GPB names only TWO verbatim: "the noble and serene **Ḥájí Mírzá Siyyid ‘Alí, the Báb's maternal uncle**" and "the pious and highly esteemed dervish, **Mírzá Qurbán-‘Alí**."

**The full Seven (Balyuzi 466, the authoritative roster):** "The other six were: **Mírzá Qurbán-‘Alí of Bárfurúsh, Ḥájí Mullá Ismá‘íl-i-Qumí, Siyyid Ḥusayn-i-Turshízí, Ḥájí Muḥammad-Taqíy-i-Kirmání, Siyyid Murtaḍá-y-i-Zanjání and Áqá Muḥammad-Ḥusayn-i-Marághi'í**" (the seventh = the Báb's uncle).

**Martyrdom at Tabríz (GPB 21310):** "**Sám Ḵhán** accordingly set out to discharge his duty… the Báb and one of his disciples, the youthful and devout **Mírzá Muḥammad-‘Alíy-i-Zunúzí, surnamed Anís**…" Then: "Sám Ḵhán… ordered his men to leave the barracks immediately, and swore, as he left the courtyard, never again, even at the cost of his life, to repeat that act. **Áqá Ján-i-Ḵhamsih, colonel of the body-guard, volunteered to replace him.**"

**Iṣfahán → Ṭihrán (DB main 21308):** the Mu‘tamid foretells "**Gurgín Ḵhán**… a ruthless successor"; after Manúchihr Khán's death Gurgín Khán "summoned **Muḥammad Big**, conveyed to him the behests of Muḥammad S̱háh, and ordered him to undertake… the journey" (the disguised transfer to the capital).

**Máh-Kú warden (DB main 21308):** "the domineering and self-willed **‘Alí Ḵhán**" relented — "the warden of the castle of Máh-Kú is to be seen associating with his captive in conditions of unrestrained freedom and friendliness."
