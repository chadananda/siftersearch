# GPB Enrichment — Bahá'u'lláh's Major Works (Baghdád → Adrianople → ‘Akká)

> Verified against corpus doc 21310 (GPB, ¶ IDs cited) + Taherzadeh ROB (429–432) for chronology + OoL best-known-works /fa/ table for Persian script. All quotes read in full paragraph context.

## 1. The Hidden Words (Kalimát-i-Maknúnih)
- type: work · TIMELINE: **1274 A.H. (~1858), Baghdád** — paced the banks of the Tigris; post-Kurdistán years, pre-Declaration.
- GPB (¶21055708): "Next to this unique repository of inestimable treasures must rank that marvelous collection of gem-like utterances, the 'Hidden Words'… Revealed in the year 1274 A.H., partly in Persian, partly in Arabic, it was originally designated the 'Hidden Book of Fáṭimih'…" Ranks **2nd only to the Kitáb-i-Íqán** among doctrinal/ethical writings of the period.
- aliases: "Hidden Book of Fáṭimih" (original title, GPB verified) · Kalimát-i-Maknúnih/Kalimat-i-Maknuniyyih · script کلمات مكنونه (likely).
- trap: "Hidden Book of Fáṭimih" = Bahá'u'lláh's original designation, which He *identified with* the prophesied Shí‘í book — not the same object. Date is A.H. not Gregorian.

## 2a. The Seven Valleys (Haft-Vádí)
- type: work · TIMELINE: **Baghdád ~1856–63**, "during that same period" as Hidden Words; reply to Shaykh Muḥyi'd-Dín, Qáḍí of Khániqayn.
- GPB (¶21055709): "…a treatise that may well be regarded as His greatest mystical composition, designated as the 'Seven Valleys,'… in which He describes the seven stages which the soul of the seeker must needs traverse…"
- significance: GPB superlative — **His greatest mystical composition**. aliases: Haft-Vádí · script هفت وادى (likely).
- trap: distinct from Four Valleys (different recipient, seven progressive stages vs four parallel paths).

## 2b. The Four Valleys (Chahár-Vádí)
- type: work · TIMELINE: **Baghdád ~1856–63**; to Shaykh ‘Abdu'r-Raḥmán-i-Karkútí, leader of the Qádiríyyih Order.
- GPB (¶21055710 / recipient ¶21055671): "The 'Four Valleys,' an epistle addressed to the learned S̱hayḵh ‘Abdu'r-Raḥmán-i-Karkútí…"
- aliases: Chahár-Vádí · Chihár-Vádí (OoL) · script چهار وادی (likely).
- trap: recipient is ‘Abdu'r-Raḥmán-i-**Karkútí** (not the Seven Valleys' Muḥyi'd-Dín of Khániqayn).

## 3. Gems of Divine Mysteries (Javáhiru'l-Asrár)
- type: work · TIMELINE: **Baghdád ~1860–62**, among writings that "poured forth from the 'Abode of Peace'." Arabic doctrinal/mystical epistle; thematically paired w/ the Íqán.
- GPB: NOT separately characterized — only in the closing enumeration (¶21055710: "…the 'Javáhiru'l-Asrár' and a host of other writings…"). Defer substance to Taherzadeh.
- aliases: Javáhiru'l-Asrár · Jawáhiru'l-Asrár · script جواهر الاسرار (likely).
- trap: don't over-state rank — GPB weight is "one of a host," not a named pre-eminent work.

## 4. Tablet of Patience — Súriy-i-Ṣabr / Lawḥ-i-Ayyúb ★ ANCHOR ★
- type: work · TIMELINE — **THE ANCHOR: first day of Riḍván, 1863 (21 Apr 1863), Garden of Riḍván, Baghdád** — the Declaration day, "the Day of supreme felicity."
- GPB (¶21055710): "…the 'Súriy-i-Ṣabr' (Súrih of Patience), revealed on the first day of Riḍván which extols Vaḥíd and his fellow-sufferers in Nayríz…"
- ROB (I, ¶6956602–03): Arabic, ~¼ length of the Íqán, in honor of **Ḥájí Muḥammad-Taqí of Nayríz, titled Ayyúb (Job)** — a follower of Vaḥíd in the Nayríz upheaval. GPB's "Vaḥíd & fellow-sufferers" + ROB's named recipient = same event via the Nayríz connection.
- aliases: Súriy-i-Ṣabr ≡ Lawḥ-i-Ayyúb (Tablet of Job) · Súrah-i-Ṣabr · script لوح صبر (لوح ايوب) (likely).
- trap: "Ayyúb/Job" = recipient's honorific title (Ḥájí Muḥammad-Taqí), NOT a tablet about Biblical Job. Vaḥíd is *extolled*, not the recipient. Revealed in Baghdád on the eve of departure.

## 5. Súriy-i-Mulúk (Tablet of the Kings)
- type: work · TIMELINE: **Adrianople ~1867**, just after the "Most Great Separation." First tablet addressing the kings *collectively*.
- GPB (¶21055769): "…the Súriy-i-Mulúk, **the most momentous Tablet revealed by Bahá'u'lláh** (Súrih of Kings) in which He, for the first time, directs His words collectively to the entire company of the monarchs of East and West…" (content ¶21055770: discloses His Mission, affirms the Báb, enjoins justice/disarmament, warns of "Divine chastisement").
- significance: GPB superlative — **"the most momentous Tablet"**; opens the Proclamation (individual ruler-tablets are its sequel).
- aliases: Súriy-i-Mulúk ≡ Súratu'l-Mulúk (Arabic construct) · Tablet of the Sovereigns · script سوره ملوك (likely).
- trap: collective address — distinct from the later *individual* ruler tablets.

## 6. Tablets to Individual Rulers (the Proclamation)
GPB: one Proclamation begun in Adrianople, consummated in ‘Akká (¶21055869). ¶21055870: the most important of these + the Súriy-i-Haykal ordered written in **pentacle** form (temple of man).
- **6a. Lawḥ-i-Napulyún I** — Adrianople; the tablet Napoleon III "cast behind his back" (¶21055852). Napoleon = "Chief of Sovereigns." script لوح اول به ناپليؤن سوم.
- **6b. Lawḥ-i-Napulyún II** — ‘Akká ~1869, barracks; GPB ¶21055852: announces "Him Who is the Unconstrained," asserts Jesus heralded His Mission, prophesies "thy kingdom shall be thrown into confusion" (fulfilled 1870). **Famous fall-prophecy is in the SECOND tablet.**
- **6c. Lawḥ-i-Malikih (Queen Victoria)** — ‘Akká ~1868–69; GPB ¶21055855: praises abolition of the slave trade + parliamentary government. Contains the "sovereign remedy"/collective-security passage (¶21055851/867).
- **6d. Lawḥ-i-Malik-i-Rús / Lawḥ-i-Qiṣar (Czar Alexander II)** — ‘Akká barracks; GPB ¶21055854: "the promised Father, Whom 'the tongue of Isaiah hath extolled'…"; acknowledges the Russian Ambassador's aid in Ṭihrán. Trap: "Qiṣar/Qayṣar" = Czar title, not a name.
- **6e. Lawḥ-i-Páp (Pope Pius IX)** — ‘Akká barracks; GPB ¶21055861: "He Who is the Lord of Lords is come overshadowed with clouds"; bids him sell the ornaments, leave his palaces.
- **6f. Sulṭán ‘Abdu'l-‘Azíz — ⚠ HUMAN REVIEW:** GPB has NO standalone characterized tablet to him — folds the Sulṭán into the Súriy-i-Mulúk (Adrianople); separately treats his ministers ‘Alí Páshá (Lawḥ-i-Ra'ís) & Fu'ád Páshá (Lawḥ-i-Fu'ád, ¶21055858). Flagged, not fabricated.
- **6g. Lawḥ-i-Sulṭán (Náṣiri'd-Dín Sháh)** — ‘Akká ~1868–69, carried on foot to Persia by 17-yr-old **Badí‘**; GPB ¶21055775: "**the lengthiest epistle to any single sovereign**." Self-disclosure passage ¶21055631 ("O King! I was but a man like others, asleep upon My couch…"). Badí‘ anchor ¶21055835 (Áqá Buzurg of Khurásán, "Pride of Martyrs"). OoL /fa/ labels it كتاب سلطان (Kitáb-i-Sulṭán) — variant.

## 7. Tablet of Aḥmad — Lawḥ-i-Aḥmad (Arabic)
- type: work · TIMELINE: **Adrianople ~1282 A.H./1865**, to Aḥmad of Yazd (reached Constantinople); before Bahá'u'lláh's poisoning by Mírzá Yaḥyá.
- GPB: listed only (¶21055769). ROB II (430) authority: ¶6006050 "one of the best known Tablets…in honour of Aḥmad, a native of Yazd"; ¶6006083 "endowed with a special potency…recite it at times of difficulty…a charter setting out the requirements of faith and servitude"; ¶6006096 "pays a moving tribute to the Báb…the King of Messengers."
- aliases: Lawḥ-i-Aḥmad · script لوح احمد (likely).
- trap: **two** Tablets of Aḥmad — Arabic (Aḥmad of Yazd, the recited one, = this) vs Persian (Aḥmad of Káshán). Don't merge.

## 8. Súriy-i-Haykal (Tablet of the Temple)
- type: work · TIMELINE: **‘Akká** (early). Bahá'u'lláh incorporated the **five ruler-tablets** within it, written in a **pentacle** (haykal = the human temple).
- GPB ¶21055630: "In His Súratu'l-Haykal… He thus describes those breathless moments when the Maiden, symbolizing the 'Most Great Spirit' proclaimed His mission… 'I heard a most wondrous, a most sweet voice…'"; ¶21055870 pentacle note.
- aliases: Súriy-i-Haykal ≡ Súratu'l-Haykal · script سوره هيكل (likely).
- trap: genitive equivalence (Persian/Arabic forms = same); "Haykal" = temple/human form (the pentacle), not a building.

## 9. The Kitáb-i-Aqdas (The Most Holy Book)
- type: work · TIMELINE: **‘Akká ~1873** (house of ‘Údí Khammár), after the Proclamation. (Characterization already enriched — this confirms the chronological slot.)
- GPB ¶21055871: "the most signal act of His ministry… 'Most Holy Book,' whose provisions must remain inviolate for no less than a thousand years… the brightest emanation of the mind of Bahá'u'lláh, … the Mother Book of His Dispensation, and the Charter of His New World Order." ¶21055872: "circa 1873… unique and incomparable among the world's sacred Scriptures."
- aliases: Kitáb-i-Aqdas ≡ al-Kitáb al-Aqdas · script كتاب اقدس (likely). The three superlatives travel together.

## 10. Epistle to the Son of the Wolf — Lawḥ-i-Ibn-i-Dhi'b
- type: work · TIMELINE: **‘Akká ~1891**, "about one year prior to His ascension" (1892); **His last major work**, to Shaykh Muḥammad-Taqí of Iṣfahán.
- GPB ¶21055887: "**the last outstanding Tablet revealed by the pen of Bahá'u'lláh**… calls upon that rapacious priest to repent… quotes some of the most characteristic and celebrated passages of His own writings…"; ¶21055888: "revealed about one year prior to His ascension." Alludes to the Kitáb-i-‘Ahd as the "Crimson Book" (¶21055924).
- aliases: Lawḥ-i-Ibn-i-Dhi'b · script لوح ابن الذئب (likely).
- trap: "Son of the Wolf" = recipient Shaykh Muḥammad-Taqí; distinct from "the Wolf" (his father Muḥammad-Báqir, Lawḥ-i-Burhán) and the "She-Serpent" (Mír Muḥammad-Ḥusayn) — three persecutor-epithets in one circle.

## 11. Tablet of Carmel — Lawḥ-i-Karmil
- type: work · TIMELINE: **‘Akká/Mt. Carmel, late ministry ~1890–91**, during a Haifa visit, tent near the Carmelite Monastery (same visit He pointed out the Báb's Shrine site to ‘Abdu'l-Bahá).
- GPB ¶21055824: "the 'Lord of the Vineyard,' revealed the Tablet of Carmel, remarkable for its allusions and prophecies"; ¶21055887: "'the City of God that hath descended from heaven'… 'erelong will God sail His Ark' upon that mountain, and 'will manifest the people of Bahá.'"
- significance: Charter of the World Spiritual & Administrative Centers on Mt. Carmel.
- aliases: Lawḥ-i-Karmil · script لوح كرمل (likely).
- trap: the *Tablet* of Carmel (Writing) ≠ Mt. Carmel (place).

## Cross-cutting
1. Súriy-i-Ṣabr = cleanest TIMELINE anchor (Riḍván Declaration day) — use as the model "date + coinciding-event" anchor.
2. Persian↔Arabic genitive equivalence confirmed: Súriy-i-Mulúk=Súratu'l-Mulúk; Súriy-i-Haykal=Súratu'l-Haykal (GPB uses both) — alias pairs, not separate entities.
3. GPB does NOT separately characterize: Gems of Divine Mysteries, Tablet of Aḥmad, Napoleon I tablet → ROB/Taherzadeh is the authority there.
4. ⚠ "Tablet to Sulṭán ‘Abdu'l-‘Azíz" needs human review (no discrete GPB tablet; folded into Súriy-i-Mulúk).
5. Two Napoleon tablets (fall-prophecy in #2); two Tablets of Aḥmad (recited one = Arabic/Yazd).
6. OoL /fa/ labels Lawḥ-i-Sulṭán as كتاب سلطان (Kitáb-i-Sulṭán) — capture as variant.
7. All script forms = confidence "likely" (corpus is English/translit only; glyphs from OoL /fa/ table).
