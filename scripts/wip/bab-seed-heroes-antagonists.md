## HEROES (non-Letter)
### Vaḥíd (Siyyid Yaḥyáy-i-Dárábí) — Bábí (martyred Nayríz 1850)
- GPB ¶26: "the trusted Siyyid Yaḥyáy-i-Dárábí, surnamed Vaḥíd, one of the most erudite, eloquent and influential of his subjects — a man who had committed to memory no less than thirty thousand traditions… completely won over"; felt "as lowly as the dust beneath His feet." ¶76: "'unique and peerless figure of his age.'" ¶132: "the erudite Vaḥíd."
- Leader of the Nayríz upheaval (Fort of Khájih). Martyred 1850: by his green turban "bound to a horse and dragged… head cut off, stuffed with straw, sent as a trophy to the Prince in S̱híráz" (¶77). Sent by Muḥammad Sháh to investigate the Báb; won over in 3 interviews. Opposed at Nayríz by Zaynu'l-‘Ábidín Khán, ‘Abdu'lláh Khán (Shujá‘u'l-Mulk), Prince Fírúz Mírzá.
- aliases: Vaḥíd ("the Unique") · Siyyid Yaḥyáy-i-Dárábí.
- ⚠ NAMESAKE TRAP: NOT Mírzá Yaḥyá (Ṣubḥ-i-Azal). Vaḥíd = Siyyid (not Mírzá), title Vaḥíd, Dárábí nisba, Nayríz martyr 1850 (vs Azal d.1912 Cyprus).

### Ḥujjat (Mullá Muḥammad-‘Alíy-i-Zanjání) — Bábí (martyred Zanján 1850–51)
- GPB ¶27: "even fiercer in zeal than Vaḥíd… An Akhbárí, a vehement controversialist, of a bold and independent temper… impatient of restraint." ¶78: "one of the ablest and most formidable champions of the Faith"; ~1,800 fellow-disciples martyred. ¶79: "the magnanimity of Ḥujjat" — lost wife & child yet exhorted forbearance; died of a wound; corpse desecrated, dragged 3 days. ¶132: "the indomitable Ḥujjat."
- Leader of the Zanján upheaval (Fort of ‘Alí-Mardán Khán, ~3,000 Bábís). Co-defender Zaynab (fought in male attire). Reinforcements ordered by the Amír-Niẓám.
- aliases: Ḥujjat ("the Proof") · Mullá Muḥammad-‘Alíy-i-Zanjání.
- ⚠ MUḤAMMAD-‘ALÍ CLUSTER: NOT Quddús (Bárfurúshí), NOT Mírzá Muḥammad-‘Alí the breaker. Pin by Ḥujjat + Zanjání + Zanján upheaval. (bare "Ḥujjat" also = common noun "proof".)

### Mullá Ṣádiq-i-Khurásání (Muqaddas / Ismu'lláhu'l-Aṣdaq) — Bahá'í (final allegiance; later Hand of the Cause)
- DB ¶230: "formerly known as Muqaddas, and surnamed by Bahá'u'lláh, Ismu'lláhu'l-Asdaq"; 5 yrs in Iṣfahán "preparing the way." Balyuzi ¶190: "That man of iron courage." (GPB gives no standalone Báb-period epithet → DB/Balyuzi supply facts.)
- FIRST to publicly proclaim the Báb's summons in Persia — sounded the adhán with the Báb-prescribed addition at Masjid-i-Naw, Shíráz (DB ¶303). Arrested w/ Quddús by Ḥusayn Khán; scourged ~900–1,000 lashes, beard burned, nose haltered, paraded, expelled (DB ¶306–7) → "the first to be persecuted on Persian soil" (jointly w/ Quddús; Basṭámí suffered earlier but in ‘Iráq). Survived to recognize Bahá'u'lláh.
- aliases: Muqaddas ("the Sanctified") · Ismu'lláhu'l-Aṣdaq (by Bahá'u'lláh) · Mullá Ṣádiq-i-Ḵhurásání.

## ANTAGONISTS (opponent; GPB balanced)
### Muḥammad Sháh (Qájár, r.1834–48) — opponent
- GPB ¶128: "a sovereign at once bigoted and irresolute who, refusing to heed the appeal of the Báb to receive Him in the capital… succumbed, at the early age of forty." ¶132: "weak alike in mind and will… rejected the overtures made to him by the Báb Himself… refused Him admittance to the capital."
- Blocked by his Grand Vizir from receiving the Báb; ordered Bahá'u'lláh's arrest after Badasht (¶109). Died 1848 → Áqásí's fall. The Báb's first Tablet to a king was to him (¶45). Father of Náṣiri'd-Dín Sháh.
- aliases: Muḥammad S̱háh.

### Ḥájí Mírzá Áqásí (Grand Vizir; real name Mírzá ‘Abbás) — opponent
- GPB ¶33: "The overbearing and crafty Ḥájí Mírzá Áqásí." ¶35: "foolish and short-sighted." ¶128: "His evil genius, the omnipotent Ḥájí Mírzá Áqásí, the power behind the throne and the chief instigator of the outrages perpetrated against the Báb… ignominiously expelled to Karbilá, falling a prey to disease, poverty and gnawing sorrow." ¶72: "into disgrace, was expelled from the capital, and sought refuge in Karbilá."
- ⚠ EPITHET PROVENANCE: "Antichrist of the Bábí Revelation" is **Balyuzi's** (doc 466 ch.9, ¶431: "a man bankrupt of ideas and bereft of graces. A native of Iravan… his real name was Mírzá ‘Abbás"), NOT GPB's. Parallels GPB's separate naming of Siyyid Muḥammad-i-Iṣfahání as "Antichrist of the Bahá'í Revelation" (different entity).
- Muḥammad Sháh's Grand Vizir; prevented the Báb–Sháh meeting; imprisoned the Báb at Máh-Kú & Chihríq. Rival of Manúchihr Khán; accomplice of Ḥusayn Khán. Disgraced 1848, died poor in Karbilá.
- aliases: Ḥájí Mírzá Áqásí · real name Mírzá ‘Abbás (of Iravan) · "His evil genius."

### Mírzá Taqí Khán, Amír-Niẓám (Amír Kabír) — opponent
- GPB ¶72 (VERIFIED — the agent missed it; I confirmed): "leaving the direction of affairs to the **obdurate, the iron-hearted Amír-Niẓám, Mírzá Taqí Ḵhán**, who, without consulting his fellow-ministers, decreed that immediate and condign punishment be inflicted on the hapless Báb[ís]." ¶128: "the **low-born and infamous** Amír-Niẓám, Mírzá Taqí Khán… directly responsible for the death-sentence of the Báb… precipitated the great upheaval of Zanján"; fate: "treacherously put to death by the royal order, his veins being opened in the bath of the Palace of Fín, near Káshán." ¶80: "the irate and murderous Amír-Niẓám." ¶85: "dispensing with an explicit order from his sovereign," sent the Báb's execution order to Prince Ḥamzih Mírzá (who refused), then to his own brother Mírzá Ḥasan Khán.
- ⚠ GPB-vs-scholarship: secular consensus = great reformer "Amír Kabír"; GPB's severe framing is PRIMARY. ALL FOUR strings now verbatim-confirmed: "obdurate"+"iron-hearted" (¶72), "low-born and infamous" (¶128), "irate and murderous" (¶80). ("bloodthirsty/reckless" was an earlier paraphrase — discard.)
- Náṣiri'd-Dín Sháh's first Grand Vizir; decreed the Báb's martyrdom (9 Jul 1850, Tabríz); drove Ṭabarsí aftermath, Seven Martyrs, Nayríz, Zanján. Executed at Fín 1852. Brother Mírzá Ḥasan Khán carried out the execution.
- aliases: Amír-Niẓám (GPB) · Amír Kabír (reformer epithet) · Atábak-i-A‘ẓam · Mírzá Taqí Khán.

### Mullá Muḥammad-i-Mámáqání (Shaykhu'l-Islám of Tabríz) — opponent
- GPB ¶41: "Mullá Muḥammad-i-Mámáqání, that one-eyed white-bearded renegade… reprimanded Him as a perverse and contemptible follower of Satan." ¶85: "who had penned the Báb's death-warrant on the very day of His examination in Tabríz."
- The Shaykhu'l-Islám who SIGNED the Báb's death warrant (1848 Tabríz examination). Akhbárí cleric.
- ⚠ DISTINCT from **Mírzá ‘Alí Aṣghar**, the OTHER Shaykhu'l-Islám of Tabríz (GPB ¶129): "the insolent, the avaricious and tyrannical Mírzá ‘Alí Aṣghar, who… applied eleven times the rods to the feet" (the bastinado) → paralysis, died that year; office abolished after him. Mámáqání = warrant; ‘Alí Aṣghar = bastinado — TWO entities. Other examination clerics (Ḥájí Mírzá Báqir, Mullá Murtaḍá-Qulí ¶85; presiding Niẓámu'l-‘Ulamá ¶41 = moderator, light record).

## PROTECTOR (other, sympathetic)
### Manúchihr Khán (the Mu‘tamadu'd-Dawlih / "the Mu‘tamid") — other
- GPB ¶31: "The wise and judicious Manúchihr Khán… a Georgian by [origin]." ¶132: "The powerful and sagacious Mu‘tamid, the one solitary figure who could have extended Him the support and protection He so sorely needed, was taken from Him by a sudden death." ¶33: the Báb "possessed the secret sympathies of the Mu‘tamid."
- Governor of Iṣfahán who sheltered & honored the Báb (DB ¶422–4): offered to consecrate his riches, win the Sháh, dismiss Áqásí; the Báb foretold his death in "three months and nine days"; willed all to the Báb; died serene. Nephew Gurgín Khán destroyed the will, seized the estate, handed the Báb to the courier Muḥammad Big.
- aliases: the Mu‘tamadu'd-Dawlih · the Mu‘tamid ("the Trusted") · Manúc̱hihr Ḵhán. (Georgian origin; "eunuch" detail = general-knowledge, lower-confidence, not in these loci.)
