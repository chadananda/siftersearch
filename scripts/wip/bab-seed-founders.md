# Báb-Period Seed — Founders (Shaykhí precursors + leading Letters of the Living)

> Verified VERBATIM vs GPB doc 21310 + Dawn-Breakers 21308. GPB sub-macron orthography captured as variants. side per dispensation/final-allegiance rule. (Anchor passage: **GPB ¶82** characterizes Quddús, Mullá Ḥusayn, Vaḥíd, Ḥujjat, Ṭáhirih together; **DB ¶208** = full 18-Letter roster.)

## 1. Shaykh Aḥmad-i-Aḥsá'í — other (herald, d.1826, before the Declaration)
- GPB ¶149: "S̱hayḵh Aḥmad-i-Aḥsá'í, **the herald of the Bábí Dispensation**, who had foreshadowed the 'strange happenings'… between the years sixty and sixty-seven… 'His Cause will be made known after Ḥín (68).'"
- DB ¶94: "that luminous Star of Divine guidance"; DB ¶88: he + Siyyid Káẓim = "those twin great lights." Held nothing short of a new independent Revelation could revive Islám. Founder of the Shaykhí school; forsook home in al-Aḥsá/Baḥrayn to unravel the Scriptures. Told Siyyid Káẓim "Neither of us shall live to gaze upon their effulgent glory" (DB ¶147).
- aliases: "herald of the Bábí Dispensation" · S̱hayḵh Aḥmad-i-Aḥsá'í (of al-Aḥsá) · script الشيخ أحمد الأحسائي (low-conf).
- rels: founder → successor Siyyid Káẓim-i-Rashtí; teacher (via writings) of the future believers; his writings reached Ṭáhirih via her Baraghání cousin.
- corpus IDs: GPB ¶149; DB ¶¶88,94,104,105,147,168. trap: pin by -i-Aḥsá'í.

## 2. Siyyid Káẓim-i-Rashtí — other (herald, d.1843, eve of Declaration)
- Not characterized at length in GPB under the Rashtí nisba. DB ¶105: "that other luminary of Divine guidance"; DB ¶88 "twin great lights." GPB ¶113: "her admiring teacher, Siyyid Káẓim" (named Ṭáhirih "Qurrat-i-‘Ayní").
- Successor head of the Shaykhí school (DB ¶120). **Deathbed dispersal charge** (DB ¶147): "Scatter far and wide, detach yourselves from all earthly things… seek and find Him who is concealed behind the veils of glory… after the Qá'im the Qayyúm will be made manifest." Named Mullá Ḥusayn "equal to this task" (DB ¶120).
- aliases: "that other luminary of Divine guidance" · Siyyid Káẓim-i-Ras̱htí (of Rasht, Gílán) · bare "Siyyid Káẓim" (→ Rashtí ONLY in Shaykhí/herald context) · script السيد كاظم الرشتي (low-conf).
- rels: student/successor of Shaykh Aḥmad; teacher of Mullá Ḥusayn, Quddús (last years, "lowliest seat" DB ¶194), Ṭáhirih.
- ⚠ NAMESAKE TRAPS (both live in-corpus): (1) **Siyyid Káẓim-i-Zanjání** = a *companion of the Báb* to Iṣfahán Sept 1846 (GPB ¶30) — different nisba; a bare "Siyyid Káẓim" in post-Declaration travel = Zanjání, not Rashtí. (2) **Ḥájí Siyyid Muḥammad-Báqir-i-Rashtí** = Iṣfahán mujtahid (DB ¶120) — shares Rashtí nisba, distinct cleric.

## 3. Mullá Ḥusayn-i-Bushrú'í (Bábu'l-Báb) — Bábí (1st to believe; martyred Ṭabarsí 1849)
- GPB ¶82 (verbatim): "**Mullá Ḥusayn, the first Letter of the Living, surnamed the Bábu'l-Báb (the Gate of the Gate); designated as the 'Primal Mirror'**… referred to… as 'beloved of My Heart'… whom the Kitáb-i-Íqán acclaimed as the one but for whom 'God would not have been established upon the seat of His mercy'… died a martyr's death at Ṭabarsí." First to recognize the Báb on the Declaration night (his account DB ¶168). Directed to Ṭihrán "which enshrines a Mystery" (→ Bahá'u'lláh, DB ¶224). Killed by ‘Abbás-Qulí Khán-i-Láríjání's bullet (GPB ¶127).
- aliases: **Bábu'l-Báb** ("Gate of the Gate") · "the Primal Mirror" · "first Letter of the Living" · "beloved of My Heart" (Báb) · Mullá Ḥusayn-i-Bus̱hrú'í (of Bushrúyih, Khurásán) · script ملا حسين بشرويه‌ای (low-conf).
- rels: student of Siyyid Káẓim; first disciple of the Báb; brother Muḥammad-Ḥasan + nephew Muḥammad-Báqir also Letters & Ṭabarsí defenders (DB ¶208); co-defender w/ Quddús.
- corpus IDs: GPB ¶¶17,44,82,127; DB ¶¶120,133,168,208,213,224,491,718.
- ⚠ TITLE TRAP (honored): **Bábu'l-Báb = Mullá Ḥusayn, NOT the Báb** — never collapse via `%báb%`. Pin by -i-Bushrú'í / first-Letter.

## 4. Quddús (Mullá Muḥammad-‘Alíy-i-Bárfurúshí) — Bábí (18th/last Letter; martyred Bárfurúsh 1849)
- GPB ¶82: "**Quddús, immortalized by Him as Ismu'lláhi'l-Ákhir (the Last Name of God); on whom… Kullu'ṭ-Ṭa‘ám later conferred… Nuqṭiy-i-Ukhrá (the Last Point); whom He elevated… to a rank second to none except… the Herald**… whom ‘Abdu'l-Bahá designated as the 'Moon of Guidance'… suffered, in the Sabzih-Maydán of Bárfurúsh, a death which even Jesus Christ… had not faced." DB ¶194: "whose name was Muḥammad-‘Alí… a direct descendant of the Imám Ḥasan… born in Bárfurús̱h… only twenty-two." His acceptance completed the 18 Letters. Conservative exponent at Badasht (GPB ¶60); co-defender at Ṭabarsí; admitted to Bahá'u'lláh's presence in Ṭihrán (DB ¶366).
- aliases: **Quddús** ("Most Holy") · **Ismu'lláhi'l-Ákhir** · **Nuqṭiy-i-Ukhrá** · "Moon of Guidance" (‘Abdu'l-Bahá) · Mullá Muḥammad-‘Alíy-i-Bárfurús̱hí (of Bárfurúsh, Mázindarán) · script قدوس / ملا محمد علی بارفروشی (low-conf).
- rels: disciple of Siyyid Káẓim then the Báb; co-leader w/ Mullá Ḥusayn at Ṭabarsí; at Badasht seated by the unveiled Ṭáhirih (GPB ¶62).
- corpus IDs: GPB ¶¶60,61,62,82; DB ¶¶133,194,301,366.
- ⚠ MUḤAMMAD-‘ALÍ CLUSTER (keep distinct): NOT **Ḥujjat = Mullá Muḥammad-‘Alíy-i-Zanjání** (GPB ¶82, Zanján); NOT **Mírzá Muḥammad-‘Alí** the Covenant-breaker (opponent). Pin Quddús by title + Bárfurúshí + last-Letter + Imám-Ḥasan descent.

## 5. Ṭáhirih (Fáṭimih Baraghání, Qurratu'l-‘Ayn) — Bábí (only woman Letter; martyred 1852)
- GPB ¶113: "A scion of the highly reputed family of Ḥájí Mullá Ṣáliḥ-i-Baraqání… **the namesake of the illustrious Fáṭimih; designated as Zarrín-Táj (Crown of Gold) and Zakíyyih (Virtuous)**… born in the same year as Bahá'u'lláh… **acclaimed as Qurrat-i-‘Ayní by… Siyyid Káẓim; entitled Ṭáhirih (the Pure One) by the 'Tongue of Power and Glory' [Bahá'u'lláh]; and the only woman enrolled by the Báb as one of the Letters of the Living.**" GPB ¶62: "the incarnation of the holy Fáṭimih," at Badasht "appeared suddenly, adorned yet unveiled." GPB ¶63 (her words): "I am the Word which the Qá'im is to utter… which shall put to flight the chiefs and nobles of the earth!" "immortal heroine" (¶106); "its greatest heroine" (¶133).
- Unveiling at **Badasht** proclaimed the break w/ Islamic law (the return-of-Fáṭimih / Bridge-of-Ṣiráṭ symbolism). Career "inaugurated in Karbilá, culminating in Badasht" (¶112). Martyred 1852 (strangled, cast into a well).
- aliases (KEEP **Fáṭimih**): **Ṭáhirih** ("the Pure One," Bahá'u'lláh) · **Qurratu'l-‘Ayn/Qurrat-i-‘Ayní** (Siyyid Káẓim) · **Zarrín-Táj** · **Zakíyyih** · "incarnation/namesake of the holy Fáṭimih" · **Fáṭimih Baraghání/Baraqání** (of Baraghán nr Qazvín) · "Persian Joan of Arc" (playwright, GPB ¶117) · script طاهره / قرة العين / فاطمه برغانی (low-conf).
- Baraghání cluster: father **Ḥájí Mullá Ṣáliḥ-i-Baraqání**; uncle/father-in-law **Ḥájí Mullá Muḥammad-Taqí Baraghání** (the "Third Martyr"); cousin who introduced her to Shaykh Aḥmad's writings then opposed the Báb out of jealousy of Mullá Ḥusayn. Husband + brothers opposed her (GPB ¶113). Teacher Siyyid Káẓim; title from Bahá'u'lláh; enrolled by the Báb; at Badasht seated by Quddús.
- corpus IDs: GPB ¶¶61,62,63,106,112,113,117,133; DB ¶208.
- ⚠ per feedback_entity_preserve_shared_names: "Fáṭimih" is shared — resolve by context (Ṭáhirih = Báb's Letter, Baraghání, Badasht), do NOT drop.
