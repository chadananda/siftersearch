## BELIEVERS (side: `Bahá'í`)

### 1. Martha Louise Root — FULL
- "archetype of Bahá'í itinerant teachers and the foremost Hand raised by Bahá'u'lláh since 'Abdu'l-Bahá's passing… Leading Ambassadress… Pride of Bahá'í teachers" (¶21056205). "the nearest approach to the example set by 'Abdu'l-Bahá Himself" (¶21056206). "this spiritually dynamic and saintly woman"; d. Honolulu 28 Sep 1939, "the fairest fruit as yet yielded by the Formative Age" (¶21056209). Bringing the Message to Queen Marie = "the most superb and by far the most momentous" service (¶21056214).
- 'Abdu'l-Bahá (quoted ¶21056213): "Thou art, in truth, a herald of the Kingdom and a harbinger of the Covenant… sowing a seed that shall… give rise to thousands of harvests." (→ bio title "Herald of the Kingdom," doc 7164.)
- 4× round the globe ~20 yrs; 400+ universities; supervised translations of Esslemont; 8 audiences w/ Queen Marie 1926–36. Posthumous **Hand of the Cause** (American Continent, doc 9768).
- aliases: "Herald of the Kingdom" · "Leading Ambassadress" · "Pride of Bahá'í teachers."
- trap: Hand status is POSTHUMOUS, not in-life. Death = Honolulu.

### 2. Thornton Chase (1847–1912) — FULL
- ⚠ **NOT named in GPB** (verified — "Chase" hits in 21310 are false positives). Characterization from 'Abdu'l-Bahá + Stockman 7179.
- 'Abdu'l-Bahá conferred title **thábit/Thahbet** ("the Firm/Steadfast") in a Tablet: "O thou firm one in the Covenant! We give thee Thahbet (the Firm) for a name." Recognized as the **first American believer** (Stockman 3786). Part of the original Chicago group under Khayru'lláh (late 1890s). Buried Inglewood CA — 'Abdu'l-Bahá visited his grave 1912 (¶21056021 mentions the Inglewood ceremony w/o naming Chase).
- aliases: Thábit / Thahbet · "first American believer."
- trap: thábit = conferred title not surname; "Thahbet" = translation variant. DO NOT fabricate a GPB quote — GPB silent on Chase.

### 3. Phoebe Apperson Hearst — FULL
- GPB ¶21055958: "Mrs. Phoebe Hearst, the well-known philanthropist (wife of Senator George F. Hearst), whom Mrs. Getsinger had… attracted to the Faith" — organized the **first Western pilgrimage to 'Akká (1898)**.
- Her testimony (¶21055961): "Those three days were the most memorable days of my life… Without a doubt 'Abbás Effendi is the Messiah of this day and generation." Later estranged (¶21055962).
- Her butler **Robert Turner** = "the first member of his race to embrace the Cause… in the West" (¶21055962), kept faith despite her estrangement.
- trap: later left the Faith — record estrangement without erasing foundational role. Husband = US Senator, not a believer.

### 4. Lua (Louisa A. Moore) Getsinger — FULL
- "that far-famed mother teacher of the West, Mrs. E. Getsinger" (¶21056168). "to Lua Getsinger, reposing forever in the capital of Egypt" (¶21056240). 'Abdu'l-Bahá titled her "Herald of the Covenant," "The Flame," "Livá/Liva" (Banner) (doc 4048).
- Won Phoebe Hearst & May Ellis Bolles; d. Cairo, reinterred in first Egyptian Bahá'í burial-ground (US State Dept aided transfer, ¶21056115).
- aliases: Lua · "Mrs. E. Getsinger" · "mother teacher of the West" · "Herald of the Covenant" · "The Flame" · "Livá/Liva."
- trap: GPB styles her by husband's initial ("Mrs. E. Getsinger") — easy to mis-key. Distinct from May Ellis (Bolles) Maxwell.

### 7. Auguste-Henri Forel — FULL (Bábí — declared)
- GPB ¶21056183: "the distinguished Swiss scientist and psychiatrist, Dr. Auguste Forel" — testament: "In 1920 I learned at Karlsruhe of the supraconfessional world religion of the Bahá'ís… I have become a Bahá'í…"; "The Bahá'í Movement… is, in my estimation, the greatest movement today working for universal peace and brotherhood." - trap: Tablet is from 'Abdu'l-Bahá, NOT Bahá'u'lláh. Forel DECLARED → classify Bábí, not other.

## ADMIRERS / ROYALTY (side: other)

- ¶21056215: "Eldest daughter of the Duke of Edinburgh… granddaughter of Czar Alexander II… she, alone among her sister-queens… was moved to spontaneously acclaim the greatness of the Message of Bahá'u'lláh." ¶21056216: "may well deserve to rank as the first of those royal supporters of the Cause of God who are to arise in the future." ¶21056230: her acknowledgment = "the first fruits of the vision which Bahá'u'lláh had seen… and had announced in His Kitáb-i-Aqdas."
- Published tributes (¶21056183/219/220/223/227): "A religion which links all creeds… the heart that beats with Divine love knows no tribe nor race"; "It is Christ's message taken up anew."

- aliases: Queen Marie of Rumania/Romania · Marie of Edinburgh. trap: capture "Rumania" (GPB) + "Romania." Public testimony, not formal enrollment → other.

This is one of the "two axes": GPB governs characterization, but an identification slip is corrected by external fact.
