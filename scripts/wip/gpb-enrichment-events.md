# GPB Enrichment — Major Events (Bahá'u'lláh → 'Abdu'l-Bahá → early Formative Age)

> type:event rows. Verified vs GPB doc 21310 (¶ = paragraph_index) + ROB vol.1 doc 429. Each links to existing person/work entities.

## 1. The Declaration of Bahá'u'lláh / Riḍván
- 21 Apr–2 May 1863 (arrived 22 Apr; departed noon 3 May), Garden of Riḍván (Najíbíyyih), across the Tigris from Baghdád. 12 days; present first 9 + last.
- GPB ¶256/262: "the holiest and most significant of all Bahá'í festivals"; "Most Great Festival," "King of Festivals," "Festival of God"; "all created things were immersed in the sea of purification." "a prelude to the final proclamation… from Adrianople" (¶256). Ended the decade "delay" since the Síyáh-Chál (¶257). Words/circumstances "shrouded in an obscurity" — only Nabíl's account survives (¶261).
- participants: Bahá'u'lláh; Mírzá Áqá Ján (gathered companions to chant the odes, ¶260); Nabíl (eyewitness); Mírzá Yaḥyá (present; foreshadows the Separation). WORK→**Súriy-i-Ṣabr** revealed day 1 (ROB ¶3785); WORK→Kitáb-i-Aqdas (calls it "Most Great Festival," ¶262).
- aliases: Riḍván · Festival of Riḍván · Most Great Festival · King of Festivals · Festival of God.
- traps: NOT the Báb's Declaration (1844 Shíráz). Garden = Najíbíyyih BAGHDÁD, not the later Riḍván Garden near ‘Akká. Event ≠ the Súriy-i-Ṣabr (work). "Declaration" alone is ambiguous — always qualify.

## 2. The Successive Banishments (the four exiles) — ARC
- Ṭihrán → Baghdád (Apr 1853) → Constantinople (16 Aug 1863) → Adrianople (12 Dec 1863) → ‘Akká (departed Adrianople 12 Aug 1868; arrived ‘Akká late Aug 1868).
- GPB ¶170 ('Abdu'l-Bahá frame): "the exile of Bahá'u'lláh in His several removes from Ṭihrán to Bag̱hdád, from thence to Constantinople, to Rumelia and to the Holy Land." Súriy-i-Mulúk ¶277: "They expelled Us… with an abasement with which no abasement on earth can compare." Mid-winter march (Nabíl ¶277): cold "nonagenarians could not recall its like." Each sovereign "more despotic" (¶258).
- participants: Bahá'u'lláh, 'Abdu'l-Bahá, Áqáy-i-Kalím, Mírzá Áqá Ján; Sulṭán ‘Abdu'l-‘Azíz (ordered Adrianople+‘Akká); Mírzá Ḥusayn Khán Mushíru'd-Dawlih (Persian Ambassador, "His calumniator," ¶272); ‘Alí & Fu'ád Páshá; Mírzá Yaḥyá+Siyyid Muḥammad. WORK→Súriy-i-Mulúk, Lawḥ-i-Ra'ís.
- traps: "Rumelia"=region containing Adrianople (not a 5th place). First remove (1853) followed the Síyáh-Chál, not an edict. Constantinople=Istanbul; Adrianople=Edirne; ‘Akká=Acre/St.Jean d'Acre (place aliases, not event).
- ⚠ SCOPE CALL: GPB's "His several removes" supports ONE arc entity; flag if user wants per-stage rows.

## 3. The Most Great Separation
- Adrianople; rupture ~Sept 1867 (withdrawal to house of Riḍá Big early 1866; public vindication at Sulṭán Salím mosque Sept 1867, ¶290).
- GPB ¶280: "the first major internal convulsion"; "More odious than… Abú-Jahl… more shameful than the betrayal of Jesus Christ by… Judas Iscariot… more infamous than even the criminal act perpetrated by Cain against Abel, the monstrous behavior of Mírzá Yaḥyá." Bahá'u'lláh named it **Ayyám-i-Shidád (Days of Stress)**, "the most great separation… irrevocably effected"; "visibly aged Him… the heaviest blow ever sustained by Him." ¶287: "open and final rupture… one of the darkest dates in Bahá'í history." ¶293: Yaḥyá = the "Most Great Idol."
- participants: Mírzá Yaḥyá (chief breaker; the poisoning left Bahá'u'lláh a shaking hand for life ¶284; poisoned well ¶285; tried to instigate Ustád Muḥammad-‘Alíy-i-Salmání to assassinate Him ¶285); Siyyid Muḥammad-i-Iṣfahání ("Antichrist of the Bahá'í Revelation," vile whisperer ¶280/282); Áqáy-i-Kalím (witness); Mírzá Áqá Ján (bore the Súriy-i-Amr); Mír Muḥammad (forced the mosque confrontation ¶290). WORK→**Súriy-i-Amr** (¶286).
- aliases: Most Great Separation · Days of Stress · Ayyám-i-Shidád · the rupture with Mírzá Yaḥyá.
- traps: NOT the Covenant-breaking under 'Abdu'l-Bahá (#7 — different breaker/period/Centre). "Most Great Idol"=Yaḥyá (title→person). Poisoning is an episode within, not a separate event.

## 4. The Proclamation to the Kings
- Opened Constantinople 1863 (lost Tablet to ‘Abdu'l-‘Azíz); main body Adrianople 1867–68; continued ‘Akká barracks 1868+.
- GPB ¶269/270/295: "the proclamation… of that same Mission to the world's secular and ecclesiastical leaders"; "in the main addressed to the kings of the earth, and to the Christian and Muslim ecclesiastical leaders." "The day-star of His Revelation was… destined to shine in its meridian glory" (¶295). "Day and night, the Divine verses were raining down in such number that it was impossible to record them" (¶296).
- participants: Bahá'u'lláh; transcribers Mírzá Áqá Ján, 'Abdu'l-Bahá, Mírzá Báqir-i-Shírází (¶296). Rulers: ‘Abdu'l-‘Azíz (first, ¶271), Napoleon III ("Chief of Sovereigns" ¶380), Alexander II (¶382), Pope Pius IX (¶389), ‘Alí Páshá (¶386), Victoria, Náṣiri'd-Dín Sháh. WORKS→Súriy-i-Mulúk, Lawḥ-i-Sulṭán (borne by Badí‘ ¶363), Lawḥ-i-Ra'ís, Lawḥ-i-Napulyún, Lawḥ-i-Páp, Lawḥ-i-Malikih.
- traps: spans 3 cities + ~a decade — don't pin to one place/date. Event ≠ individual ruler-tablets (works). "Súriy-i-Mulúk" = both a tablet and a touchstone of this event.

## 5. Imprisonment in the ‘Akká Barracks + Martyrdom of the Purest Branch
- ‘Akká barracks/citadel; confined ~Aug/Sept 1868–Nov 1870. Purest Branch d. **23 Jun 1870**.
- GPB: ‘Akká "a penal colony to which murderers, highway robbers and political agitators were consigned" (¶332); "the most desolate of the cities… as though it were the metropolis of the owl" (Lawḥ-i-Sulṭán). Bahá'u'lláh designated it the **"Most Great Prison"** (¶330, refusing the name even for the Síyáh-Chál). First night: "all were deprived of either food or drink… begged for water, and were refused" (¶334); "From the foundation of the world… a cruelty such as this hath neither been seen nor heard of."
- Purest Branch (¶336): "the noble, the pious Mírzá Mihdí, the Purest Branch, ‘Abdu'l-Bahá's twenty-two year old brother, an amanuensis… fell through the unguarded skylight onto a wooden crate… which pierced his ribs… twenty-two hours later, his death." Dying prayer: "that his life might be accepted as a ransom." Bahá'u'lláh (¶337): "I have, O my Lord, offered up that which Thou hast given Me, that Thy servants may be quickened" — ranked beside Abraham's sacrifice, the crucifixion, the martyrdom of Imám Ḥusayn.
- participants: Bahá'u'lláh; Mírzá Mihdí (martyr; remains later to Monument Gardens, Mt Carmel ¶338); 'Abdu'l-Bahá + family; ‘Abdu'l-‘Azíz (the Farmán); pilgrims turned away at the moat — Ḥájí Abu'l-Ḥasan-i-Ardikání, Ustád Ismá‘íl-i-Káshí, Nabíl (¶335).
- traps: barracks (1868–70) distinct from later eased house/Bahjí confinement — "Most Great Prison" = the strict first phase. Purest Branch died by FALL (martyrdom/sacrifice), not execution. Mírzá Mihdí ≠ Mírzá Muḥammad-‘Alí.
- ⚠ SCOPE CALL: consider a SEPARATE event row for the Martyrdom of the Purest Branch (GPB ¶336–338 full treatment) — user's call.

## 6. The Ascension of Bahá'u'lláh
- dawn **29 May 1892** (2 Dhi'l-Qa‘dih 1309), Mansion of Bahjí, 75th year; interred after sunset same day in the adjoining house (¶421).
- GPB ¶419: contracted fever 8 May, "His ascension, at the hour of dawn… (May 29, 1892)… winged its flight to His 'other dominions.'" ¶425: "draws to a close a period which, in many ways, is unparalleled in the world's religious history." Telegram to Sulṭán ‘Abdu'l-Ḥamíd opened "The Sun of Bahá has set" (¶421).
- significance: activated the Covenant — the sealed **Kitáb-i-‘Ahd (Book of My Covenant)** entrusted to 'Abdu'l-Bahá, read after; appointed Him Centre (¶451/455).
- participants: Bahá'u'lláh; 'Abdu'l-Bahá (recipient, "now represented the Cause" ¶423); Mírzá Muḥammad-‘Alí ("Greater Branch," ranked beneath, ¶455 — seed of #7); Nabíl (drowned himself in grief; compiled the Tablet of Visitation ¶422). WORK→**Kitáb-i-‘Ahd** (¶455 "excellent and priceless Heritage").
- aliases: Ascension/passing of Bahá'u'lláh · "the Sun of Bahá has set."
- traps: "Ascension"=Bahá'í term for death of a Central Figure (not bodily ascent); same term for 'Abdu'l-Bahá (#10). Place = Bahjí not the barracks. Kitáb-i-‘Ahd = a work unsealed AT this event. Shrine at Bahjí = a place entity.

## 7. The Covenant-Breaking Crisis under 'Abdu'l-Bahá (Rebellion of Mírzá Muḥammad-‘Alí)
- ‘Akká/Holy Land; from 1892 (immediately after the ascension), dominated the first decade-plus.
- GPB ¶461: spread "grief and bewilderment… inspire its vigilant and redoubtable adversaries with fresh hope." ¶471: backed by "the powerful and perfidious Jamál-i-Burújirdí," they "arose, as one man, to launch a campaign of abuse… represented ‘Abdu'l-Bahá as an ambitious, a self-willed, an unprincipled and pitiless usurper." ¶609–610: Muḥammad-‘Alí "showed a hatred more unrelenting" — the "arch-breaker of Bahá'u'lláh's Covenant." ¶558: the Covenant's "decisive triumph over the dark forces."
- participants: Mírzá Muḥammad-‘Alí (arch-breaker, named beneath 'Abdu'l-Bahá in the Kitáb-i-‘Ahd, which he repudiated); 'Abdu'l-Bahá (target/vindicated); Jamál-i-Burújirdí + henchmen Ḥájí Ḥusayn-i-Káshí, Khalíl-i-Khu'í, Jalíl-i-Tabrízí (¶471); Mírzá Badí‘u'lláh (later led the Shrine-seizure attempt ¶618). WORK→Kitáb-i-‘Ahd, Tablet of the Branch, the Will & Testament (#10).
- traps: NOT the Most Great Separation (#3 — Yaḥyá, 1860s, vs Bahá'u'lláh). Ghuṣn-i-Akbar "Greater Branch" (Muḥammad-‘Alí, lesser station) ≠ Ghuṣn-i-A‘ẓam "Most Great Branch" ('Abdu'l-Bahá). Distinct from the external crisis (Commission of Inquiry ~1901–08, ¶497). Muḥammad-‘Alí breaker ≠ Quddús/Hujjat.

## 8. 'Abdu'l-Bahá's Western Travels
- 1910–1913: Egypt (from 1910), Europe (first Western public address 10 Sep 1911, City Temple London; Paris; Germany/Austria/Hungary), North America (8-month US/Canada tour 1912).
- GPB ¶535: "His three years of travel… mark… a turning point of the utmost significance in the history of the century." ¶537: "expounded… for the first time in His ministry, those basic and distinguishing principles." ¶548: America "the most astonishing manifestation of the boundless vitality." ¶557: "marked the culmination of His ministry."
- significance: first in-person proclamation in the West by a Central Figure; laid the dedication stone of the Wilmette Mashriqu'l-Adhkár (¶549); addressed Columbia/Howard/NYU, NAACP 4th conference, Lake Mohonk Peace Conferences (¶551); preached racial unity.
- participants: 'Abdu'l-Bahá; hosts Lady Blomfield, Rev. R.J. Campbell, Archdeacon Wilberforce, Khedive ‘Abbás Ḥilmí Páshá II + Lord Kitchener (¶539), Mrs Thornburgh-Cropper (¶493). Leads into the Tablets of the Divine Plan (#9). WORKS→Promulgation of Universal Peace, Paris Talks.
- traps: GPB dates start to 1910 (Egypt); "1911–1913" = the Europe/America legs — capture both. Egypt sojourns are part of this event. ‘Abbás Ḥilmí Páshá II (Khedive/host) ≠ ‘Abbás Effendi (='Abdu'l-Bahá) — name-coincidence trap.

## 9. The Tablets of the Divine Plan (event + sibling work)
- Revealed Holy Land **26 Mar 1916–8 Mar 1917** (14 tablets); unveiled "Convention of the Covenant," Hotel McAlpin NY, **Apr 1919** (dates web-sourced; GPB gives the frame + unveiling year).
- GPB ¶580: 'Abdu'l-Bahá "moved to confer once again, and for the last time… on the community of His American followers a signal mark of His special favor by investing them… through the revelation of the Tablets of the Divine Plan, with a world mission, whose full implications even now… still remain undisclosed." Shoghi Effendi's standing term: the "mandate"/"supreme charter for teaching." ¶734: Martha Root "first to arise… the very year the Tablets… were unveiled."
- participants: 'Abdu'l-Bahá; the American/Canadian community (addressees); Martha Root (first responder); Shoghi Effendi (built the Plans upon them). WORK→the 14 Tablets.
- traps: revelation (1916–17) ≠ unveiling (1919) — war kept them in the vault under the Shrine of the Báb. 1919 "Convention of the Covenant" ≠ the founding Covenant (Kitáb-i-‘Ahd #6). Same name = both event and work — link but keep distinct. The 1916/17/19 dates are web-verified, NOT in GPB.

## 10. The Ascension of 'Abdu'l-Bahá
- ~1:15 A.M. **28 Nov 1921**, 78th year, His house in **Haifa** (¶589/591). Funeral 29 Nov, ~10,000 mourners; interred in a room of the Shrine of the Báb, Mt Carmel (¶595–597).
- GPB ¶591 (last words, offered food): "'You wish Me to take some food, and I am going?' A minute later His spirit had winged its flight to its eternal abode." ¶595: "a funeral the like of which Palestine had never seen… ten thousand people," **Sir Herbert Samuel** attending "to express my respect for His creed and my regard for His person"; 9 Muslim/Jewish/Christian speakers (¶597). ¶616: the Will & Testament "the offspring resulting from that mystic intercourse between" Bahá'u'lláh and its "sole Interpreter." ¶617: the Administrative Order "unique in the annals of the world's religious systems."
- significance: close of the Heroic/Apostolic Age, inception of the **Formative Age**. The **Will & Testament** appointed **Shoghi Effendi** Guardian (Valí Amru'lláh), established the Guardianship + Universal House of Justice + Administrative Order (¶615). Mírzá Badí‘u'lláh's Shrine-seizure attempt "came to naught" (¶618).
- participants: 'Abdu'l-Bahá; Shoghi Effendi (Guardian); the Greatest Holy Leaf; Sir Herbert Samuel (High Commissioner, mourner, side=other); Mírzá Badí‘u'lláh (failed seizure ¶618). WORK→**Will & Testament of 'Abdu'l-Bahá** (Charter of the Administrative Order, ¶616–617); links to Kitáb-i-‘Ahd (#6) + Divine Plan (#9).
- aliases: Ascension/passing of 'Abdu'l-Bahá · inception of the Guardianship · birth of the Administrative Order.
- traps: Haifa/1921 ≠ Bahjí/1892 (#6); both "ascension." Will & Testament = a work unsealed at this event. The Formative Age / Administrative Order may merit its own era/concept entity linked here. Sir Herbert Samuel = external official (other), not a believer.
