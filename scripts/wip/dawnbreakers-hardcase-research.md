# Dawn-Breakers Hard-Case People Research — Evidence + Recommendations

**Purpose:** Gather corpus evidence + a best-supported recommendation on six open Dawn-Breakers
people-identity cases, for the USER (who knows the book intimately) to adjudicate. NOT a decision —
evidence + recommendation + the residual open question per case.

**Method:** Corpus-first, consonant-skeleton `LIKE`, paragraphs READ (not just grep-counted).
Sources scoped to: DB main **21308** + footnotes **40108**; GPB **21310**; Balyuzi *The Báb* **466**;
Ahdieh *Nayríz Heroes* **11375**; Rabbani *Bábís of Nayriz* **16552/11344** + *Family of Vaḥíd* **11242**;
plus Ahdieh *Awakening* 20331, eyewitness 1909 account 15643. Locus = `doc_id:para_id`.

**Authority order:** GPB (21310) > Nabíl main text (21308) > footnotes (40108, "various recollections,
spelling often off"). Rabbani/Ahdieh are scholarly histories, authoritative for facts/dates the DB leaves bare.

## CASE 1 — Mírzá Na‘ím: ONE man (recommendation: ONE)

**What the sources say:**

- **DB main 21308:21054956** — Zaynu'l-‘Ábidín Khán's widow "pressed Mírzá Na‘ím, who held the reins of
  authority in his grasp and was then residing in S̱híráz, to avenge the blood of her husband." (= the
  1853 / Nayríz-II avenger, residing in Shíráz.)
- **DB footnote 40108:21656845** — "...led her to Mirza Na'im, who was then on Mount Biyaban seated upon a
  stone" — the same 1853 commander on campaign.
- **Rabbani 16552:16913248 / 16913321 / 16913446–16913597** — extensive: "Mírzá Na‘ím Lashkar-Nivís,
  governor of Nayríz" leads the army in Nayríz-II (1853), captures ~150, parades severed heads to Shíráz.
- **Rabbani 16552:16913221** — "Mírzá Muhammad-Na‘ím Núrí, the son of Muhammad-Zakí Khán, led a large army."
- **Rabbani 16552:16913438** — the Prime Minister "had given the governorship of Nayríz to Mírzá Na‘ím,
  who in turn had passed it on to [Zaynu'l-‘Ábidín] Khán." (So Na‘ím held the governorship FIRST, pre-1853,
  and handed it to Zaynu'l-‘Ábidín Khán — this is the pre-1853 sequence, NOT 1909.)
- **THE DECISIVE LOCUS — Rabbani 16552:16913439**, quoting *Táríkh Zuhúru'l-Haqq* vol.4 quoting
  *Fársnámih Násirí*: **"Mírzá Na‘ím, the Lashkar-Báshí, was born in Shiraz in 1219 A.H. [1804] into one
  of the Núrí families residing in Shiraz... For a while he was the governor of Nayríz, Dáráb, Jahrum...
  and eventually died in Shiraz in the year 1292 A.H. [1875]."**
- **Rabbani 16552:16913440** — "Mírzá Na‘ím was a brother-in-law and a cousin of the ruling Prime Minister
  Mírzá Áqá Khán Núrí."

**The 1909 question (resolving the framing in the task):** Nayríz-III (1909) was led by **Shaykh
Dhakariyya / Shaykh Zakaria**, NOT Mírzá Na‘ím (eyewitness account 15643:16258536, 16258553, 16258585;
Ahdieh 11375:11301426, 11301473). There is NO 1909 Mírzá Na‘ím in the corpus. The task's framing that
Rabbani 16552 places Na‘ím "in the 1909-era material" appears to be a mis-read: 16552 narrates Nayríz-II
(1853); its "handed the governorship to Zaynu'l-‘Ábidín Khán" detail is the pre-1853 setup, not 1909.

**RECOMMENDATION: ONE man.** Mírzá (Muḥammad-)Na‘ím Núrí, Lashkar-Nivís(-Báshí) / Lashkar-Báshí, son of
Muḥammad-Zakí Khán, born Shíráz 1219 A.H. (1804), died Shíráz 1292 A.H. (1875). Governor of Nayríz/Dáráb/
Jahrum; commander of the royal force in the 1853 Nayríz-II suppression; brother-in-law & cousin of PM
Mírzá Áqá Khán Núrí. The DB "avenger residing in Shíráz" and the Rabbani "governor/commander" are the
same single career official. He is `opponent`. His career is 1850s–1870s, NOT 1850s–1900s.

**OPEN QUESTION FOR USER:** The dates (1804–1875) put him squarely in one lifetime, ruling out a
50-years-apart namesake. Do you have any independent recollection of a *separate* Na‘ím tied to the 1909
events? Corpus says 1909 = Shaykh Dhakariyya, so unless your knowledge contradicts the Fársnámih dates,
this is one man and the "two Na‘íms ~50 yrs apart" worry dissolves.

## CASE 2 — The Sulaymán Kháns: at least THREE distinct men (+ the martyr's brother as a 4th figure)

The corpus uses "Sulaymán" for several unrelated men. Laid out with father / nisba / role / fate:

**(A) Ḥájí Sulaymán Khán — the famous 1852 martyr. `Bábí`.**
- Father: **Yaḥyá Khán** (an officer of the Náyibu's-Salṭanih who was Muḥammad Sháh's father, i.e. ‘Abbás
  Mírzá) — DB 21308:21054902. Balyuzi 466:6716163 styles him **"Sulaymán Khán, the son of Yaḥyá Khán
  *of Tabríz*"** → hence the nisba **-i-Tabrízí**.
- Role/arc: had no taste for court; lived near the Shrine of Imám Ḥusayn in ‘Iráq; follower of Siyyid Káẓim
  then the Báb; **attained the Báb's presence in Chihríq in disguise** (Balyuzi 466:6716163); **recovered
  the Báb's remains after the martyrdom** and hid them in the Mílán silk factory (DB 21308:21054717,
  21054719; GPB 21310:21055992).
- Fate: 1852 reprisals after the attempt on the Sháh — martyred by candles thrust into nine holes in his
  flesh and burned (DB 21308:21054902–21054912; GPB 21310:21055592). Footnote 40108:21656799 (Samandar):
  "Sulayman Khan attained to the presence of the Bab in the course of His pilgrimage to Mecca and Medina."
  → THIS is the famous Bábí hero.

**(B) Sulaymán Khán-i-Afshár(-i-Shahríyárí) — the opposing GENERAL at Shaykh Ṭabarsí. `opponent`.**
- Nisba: **-i-Afshár-i-Shahríyárí** (the Afshár tribe). Distinct family from (A).
- Role: enemy commander seconding Prince Mihdí-Qulí Mírzá at Ṭabarsí (DB 21308:21054471, 21054490,
  21054510); footnote 40108:21656232 (Nicolas) — "Sulayman Khan-i-Afshar, recently sent by the Shah,
  was urging" a treacherous stratagem against the besieged.
- Likely the SAME Afshár who later carried the order to move the Báb from Chihríq to Tabríz (footnote
  40108:21656509, Gobineau: "Sulayman Khan, the Afshar... order to take the Bab out of the fort of Chihriq").

**(C) Ḥájí Sulaymán Khán-i-Afshár (Zanjan) — the official who ignored the Báb's letter. `opponent`/`other`.**
- The one to whom the Báb sent a message at Siyáh-Dihán via Mullá Iskandar; he "did not heed it and left
  for the capital" (Balyuzi 466:6716087–6716088; DB 21308:21054212 "Sulaymán Ḵhán-i-Afs̱hár... a great
  admirer of the late Siyyid Káẓim"). Father of **Riḍá-Qulí Khán-i-Afshár**, the officer sent to move the
  Báb from Máh-Kú (Balyuzi 466:7498483). Note: (B) and (C) are both "Afshár"; the corpus may or may not
  intend them as one — see open question.

**(D) Farrukh Khán — NOT a Sulaymán, but the likely source of the "one hero / one opponent" framing.**
- DB 21308:21054795: "**Farruḵh Ḵhán, son of Yaḥyá Ḵhán and brother of Ḥájí Sulaymán Ḵhán**, one of the
  generals of the enemy's army" — died at Zanján. So the martyr (A) had a *brother* who was an enemy
  general. The "Bábí hero vs. opponent, both sons of the same father" pattern is martyr (A) **Sulaymán** vs.
  brother (D) **Farrukh** — they are brothers, not two Sulaymáns.

**Two traps to note:**
- "Yaḥyá Khán" is itself a shared name. The martyr's father (A) is a Tihrán/Tabríz court officer. The
  **warden of Chihríq** is a DIFFERENT man, **Yaḥyá Khán-i-Kurd**, whose sister married Muḥammad Sháh
  (DB 21308:21054325; Balyuzi 466:6716113–6716114). The corpus gives the Chihríq warden NO son named
  Sulaymán. The martyr merely *visited* Chihríq as a pilgrim; he is not the warden's son.
- "Sulaymán" also appears as the place **Sulaymáníyyih** (Kurdistán) and **Sulaymán-i-Ghannám** (owner of
  the Most Great House) and **Mírzá Sulaymán-i-Núrí** (Badasht chanter) — all unrelated; prune these.

**RECOMMENDATION:**
- The famous 1852 martyr = **Ḥájí Sulaymán Khán-i-Tabrízí, son of Yaḥyá Khán** (`Bábí`). He is the one
  who recovered the Báb's remains and was martyred by candles.
- The Ṭabarsí opposing general = **Sulaymán Khán-i-Afshár-i-Shahríyárí** (`opponent`) — a distinct man,
  Afshár tribe, no Bábí allegiance.
- These two are cleanly split by **nisba (Tabrízí/son-of-Yaḥyá vs. Afshár-i-Shahríyárí) + role + fate**.
  The "Bábí hero vs. battlefield opponent who are kin" intuition is real but it's the martyr Sulaymán (A)
  vs. his brother Farrukh Khán (D), not two men both named Sulaymán.

**OPEN QUESTION FOR USER:**
1. The task's framing said both were "sons of **‘Alí Khán** of Tabríz." The corpus consistently says the
   martyr's father is **Yaḥyá Khán** (‘Alí Khán is the unrelated Máh-Kú warden). Do you have a source
   naming an ‘Alí Khán as father? — likely a memory slip, but worth your confirmation.
2. Are the Zanjan Afshár (C, ignored-the-letter, father of Riḍá-Qulí) and the Ṭabarsí Afshár general (B)
   ONE Afshár official or two? The corpus doesn't explicitly merge or split them; both are "Sulaymán Khán-i-
   Afshár." Your call on whether to keep them as one entity with a role-arc or two namesakes.

## CASE 3 — Sang-Sar / Abú-Ṭálib tangle (Shaykh Ṭabarsí): TWO families, one survivor, plus a Nayríz namesake to wall off

**Family A — the siyyid family of Mír Muḥammad-‘Alí (a Shaykhí scholar of Sang-Sar):**
- **DB 21308:21054515** — father **Mír Muḥammad-‘Alí** took his two sons to Karbilá to meet Siyyid Káẓim;
  dreamed both sons "**Siyyid Aḥmad and Mír Abu'l-Qásim** would attain the Qá'im and each suffer martyrdom."
  → names **Mír Abu'l-Qásim** as Aḥmad's brother **"who met his death the very night on which Mullá Ḥusayn
  was slain."** ← THIS resolves the bare "Mír Abu'l-Qásim (died the night Mullá Ḥusayn fell)."
- **DB 21308:21054542** — **"Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib-i-Sang-Sárí, one of those who survived"** wrote a
  communication to Bahá'u'lláh relating "his own story, as well as that of his two brothers, **Siyyid Aḥmad
  and Mír Abu'l-Qásim, both of whom were martyred** while defending the fort." His father = **Mír
  Muḥammad-‘Alí**. ← THIS resolves the bare "Mírzá/Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib (survivor)" = Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib-i-Sang-Sárí.
- **DB 21308:21054847** — Nabíl's source for the Mázandarán struggle was "a certain **Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib-i-
  S̱hahmírzádí**" who sent a written account to the Holy Land. Shahmírzád is the town adjacent to Sang-Sar;
  this is almost certainly the SAME survivor (the written-account author of 21054542). Rabbani 16552:16913116
  independently cites "Mírzá Abú-Tálib Shahmírzádí... unpublished eyewitness account" — corroborates that
  the survivor/chronicler = Abú-Ṭálib of Sang-Sar / Shahmírzád.

**Family B — the non-siyyid sons of Karbilá'í Abú-Muḥammad:**
- **DB 21308:21054516** — "Karbilá'í Abú-Muḥammad urged his two sons, **Abu'l-Qásim and Muḥammad-‘Alí**,
  to arise."
- **DB 21308:21054517** — "These **two sons of Karbilá'í Abú-Muḥammad** were the two companions who had
  been ushered, together with Siyyid Aḥmad, into the presence of the prince." → the "three companions of
  Quddús, residents of Sang-Sar" of 21054515 = Siyyid Aḥmad (Family A) + Abu'l-Qásim & Muḥammad-‘Alí (Family B).

**INTERNAL NABÍL TENSION on Siyyid Aḥmad's fate (flag for user):**
- DB 21308:21054519 says Siyyid Aḥmad was **martyred** (Mírzá Muḥammad-Taqí "cut his body to pieces"),
  while "his brother **Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib... to this day resides with his brother Siyyid Muḥammad-Riḍá** in
  Mázindarán" (survived).
- DB 21308:21054542 likewise has Aḥmad **martyred**, Abú-Ṭálib **survived**.
- BOTH DB passages agree Aḥmad was martyred and Abú-Ṭálib survived. **This contradicts the task note that
  "DB confirms... Siyyid Aḥmad survived."** I could not locate any DB passage where Aḥmad survives — the
  text I read martyrs him twice. (Possible the "survived" note refers to a different source or to Abú-Ṭálib.)

**How many DISTINCT people (Sang-Sar / Ṭabarsí cluster):**
1. **Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib-i-Sang-Sárí** (= "Mírzá Abú-Ṭálib" survivor = "Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib-i-Shahmírzádí"
   chronicler) — Family A, **survivor**, son of Mír Muḥammad-‘Alí. `Bábí`.
2. **Siyyid Aḥmad** — Family A, brother of #1, **martyred** (per DB). `Bábí`.
3. **Mír Abu'l-Qásim** (= "Mír Abu'l-Qásim-i-Sang-Sárí") — Family A, brother of #1 & #2, **martyred the
   night Mullá Ḥusayn fell**. `Bábí`. ← the bare "Mír Abu'l-Qásim" = this man.
4. **Siyyid Muḥammad-Riḍá** — Family A, the brother Abú-Ṭálib "resides with" (21054519); possibly a 4th
   brother who also survived. `Bábí`.
5. **Abu'l-Qásim, son of Karbilá'í Abú-Muḥammad** — Family B (NOT a siyyid), distinct from #3. Martyr. `Bábí`.
6. **Muḥammad-‘Alí, son of Karbilá'í Abú-Muḥammad** — Family B. Martyr. `Bábí`.

**Bare-name → nisba'd mapping:**
- bare "Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib" (brother of Siyyid Aḥmad) = **Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib-i-Sang-Sárí** (#1, survivor).
- bare "Mírzá Abú-Ṭálib" (survivor) = same #1 (Nabíl also styles him "Shahmírzádí"; "Mírzá" vs "Siyyid"
  is a styling drift — same chronicler-survivor).
- bare "Mír Abu'l-Qásim" (died night Mullá Ḥusayn fell) = **Mír Abu'l-Qásim-i-Sang-Sárí** (#3, Family A).

**A NAMESAKE TO WALL OFF (do NOT merge into the Sang-Sar cluster):**
- **(Áqá) Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib of NAYRÍZ** — the **kad-khudá of the Bázár quarter, a companion of Vaḥíd**
  (DB 21308:21054650), "very wealthy, bound with chains and sent by the governor of Nayriz to Ma'dan, and
  there poisoned" (footnote 40108:21656497). Different town (Nayríz, not Sang-Sar), different episode
  (1850 Nayríz-I), different fate (poisoned). This is a SEPARATE person from the Sang-Sar survivor — guard
  against a `%Abú-Ṭálib%` collision.

**RECOMMENDATION:** Six distinct people in the Sang-Sar/Ṭabarsí cluster (two families), per the roster
above; plus a seventh, unrelated Nayríz Siyyid Abú-Ṭálib to keep apart. Treat #1 (the survivor-chronicler)
as ONE entity even though Nabíl styles him variously "Siyyid"/"Mírzá" Abú-Ṭálib "-i-Sang-Sárí"/"-i-Shahmírzádí."

**OPEN QUESTION FOR USER:** (a) Your note says "DB confirms... Siyyid Aḥmad survived" — but both DB
passages I read MARTYR him (21054519, 21054542) and have ABÚ-ṬÁLIB as the survivor. Can you confirm which
is right — is the survivor Abú-Ṭálib (DB) or did you mean Aḥmad? (b) Is "Siyyid Muḥammad-Riḍá" (the brother
Abú-Ṭálib resides with) a genuine 4th brother, or a confusion? (c) The "18 Sang-Sar martyrs" count — which
source? I did not find it in DB 21308/40108; the "eighty" and "eighteen" hits in the corpus refer to other
things (eighteen days without food at Ṭabarsí, eighty captives at Nayríz).

## CASE 4 — Mullá Muḥammad-i-Ḥamzih: ONE man, mis-tagged (recommendation: ONE; tag `other`, not `Bábí`)

**What the sources say (both describe the same anti-violence Bárfurúsh divine):**
- **DB main 21308:21054541** — "**Ḥájí Mullá Muḥammad-i-Ḥamzih, surnamed the S̱harí‘at-Madár**, succeeded
  in burying their bodies [of two slain Bábís] in the neighbourhood of the Masjid-i-Káẓim-Big, and in
  inducing their murderer to repent and ask forgiveness."
- **DB footnote 40108:21656265** (Browne / Hájí Mírzá Jání) — "a pious divine **Haji Muhammad-'Aliy-i-
  Hamzih** by name, whose skill in exegesis and spiritual gifts was recognised by all, secretly sent several
  persons to bury the mutilated remains [of Quddús]... far from approving the Sa'idu'l-'Ulama''s conduct,
  used to curse and revile him, and **never himself pronounced sentence of death against any Babi**, but
  on the contrary used to obtain decent burial for those slain."

Both = the Bárfurúsh ‘ulamá who buried martyred Bábís, opposed the Sa‘ídu'l-‘Ulamá's violence, and never
condemned a Bábí. The footnote's "Muḥammad-‘Alíy-i-Ḥamzih" is a transliteration/name-element variant of the
main text's "Mullá Muḥammad-i-Ḥamzih, the Sharí‘at-Madár" (the footnotes are "various recollections,
spelling often off"). No second, separately-Bábí Ḥamzih exists in the corpus.

**Critical point:** he was a **sympathetic Muslim divine, NOT a Bábí.** He buried Bábí dead and refused to
condemn them, but the sources never make him a believer. So the **`Bábí` tag on "Ḥájí Mullá Muḥammad-i-
Ḥamzih" is the error** — both records are the same man and both should be `other` (sympathizer), not `Bábí`.

**RECOMMENDATION: ONE man, mis-tagged.** Merge the two records into a single entity: Ḥájí Mullá Muḥammad-i-
Ḥamzih, the Sharí‘at-Madár of Bárfurúsh; aliases include the footnote's "Ḥájí Muḥammad-‘Alíy-i-Ḥamzih."
Tag `other`. Note the divergent footnote spelling as a captured variant.

**OPEN QUESTION FOR USER:** Do you read the footnote's "Muḥammad-‘Alí" as a genuinely different given name
(a brother/relative also named Ḥamzih) rather than a spelling drift of the same man? The role described is
identical (burying Quddús's/Bábís' remains, reviling the Sa‘ídu'l-‘Ulamá), so I judge them one — but the
"-‘Alí" element is the only thread that could argue for two.

## CASE 5 — Mír Siyyid Muḥammad of Iṣfahán: DISTINCT figure — the Báb's host, the Imám-Jum‘ih / Sulṭánu'l-‘Ulamá (NOT the Covenant-breaker namesake)

**The Báb's-life-arc figure:**
- **Balyuzi 466:6716039** — names him outright: "**Siyyid Muḥammad, the Sulṭánu'l-‘Ulamá, the Imám-Jum‘ih
  of Iṣfahán**," to whom Manúchihr Khán sent the Báb's letter requesting shelter; he welcomed the Báb with
  reverence and **hosted Him in his own home** (1846).
- **DB 21308:21054165–21054173** — the Báb at Iṣfahán, lodged via the Mu‘tamid (Manúchihr Khán) and the
  **Imám-Jum‘ih**, in whose house "an unceasing stream of visitors flowed."
- **GPB 21310:21055505** — Ḥájí Mírzá Áqásí "severely upbraided the **Imám-Jum‘ih** for the neglect of his
  sacred duty," suspecting him of sympathy for the Báb.
This is the Báb's Iṣfahán protector/host — a real, distinct historical figure (the Sulṭánu'l-‘Ulamá).

**The namesake to keep apart (NOT the same man):**
- **Siyyid Muḥammad-i-Iṣfahání** the Covenant-breaker — "that living embodiment of wickedness, cupidity
  and deceit" who corrupted Mírzá Yaḥyá (GPB 21310:21055755). Bahá'u'lláh's enemy in Baghdad/Adrianople,
  decades later. Entirely different person, different period, different role.
- A third, minor "**Siyyid Muḥammad-i-Iṣfahání**" appears merely as a witness present in Tihrán in 1265 A.H.
  (DB 21308:21054379) — likely yet another bearer of the common name; possibly the future Covenant-breaker,
  but unconfirmed.

**RECOMMENDATION: the "Báb's-life-arc" Mír/Siyyid Muḥammad of Iṣfahán is a DISTINCT, real figure — the
Imám-Jum‘ih, the Sulṭánu'l-‘Ulamá of Iṣfahán, the Báb's host (1846).** It is NOT an echo/alias of another
cleric; it must be kept strictly separate from the Covenant-breaker Siyyid Muḥammad-i-Iṣfahání (a `%Siyyid
Muḥammad%Iṣfahán%` collision is the danger). Tag the host `other` (a sympathetic Muslim dignitary, never a
declared believer); tag the Covenant-breaker `opponent`.

**OPEN QUESTION FOR USER:** Do your records carry the host specifically as "**Mír** Siyyid Muḥammad" (the
"Mír" prefix)? Balyuzi/DB/GPB style him "Siyyid Muḥammad, the Sulṭánu'l-‘Ulamá / the Imám-Jum‘ih," without
"Mír." Confirm the "Mír Siyyid Muḥammad" form maps to the Imám-Jum‘ih and not to some third Iṣfahán siyyid
you have in view.

## CASE 6 — Mullá ‘Abdu'l-Ḥusayn of Nayríz: ONE bridging figure (1850 wounded → 1853 martyred), NOT two

**What the sources say — the same "man of eighty" runs through BOTH the 1850 and 1853 episodes:**
- **DB 21308:21054650 (NAYRÍZ-I, 1850)** — "The first to suffer was that same **Mullá ‘Abdu'l-Ḥusayn** who,
  despite his advanced age, had walked out to welcome Vaḥíd. He was offering his prayer on the roof of his
  house when a bullet struck his right foot." Vaḥíd wrote consoling him. (= DB's "man of eighty," 1850.)
- **Ahdieh 11375:11301482 (1850)** — "**Eighty-year-old Mullá ‘Abdu'l Husayn** and his son... traveled 40
  miles to Ronize from Nayriz to meet Vahid... became one of the leaders of the Bábí movement... shot in the
  leg by one of the governor's soldiers... Vahid... referred to Husayn as 'the **First Martyr of Nayriz**'
  even though he survived." (Survived 1850.)
- **Rabbani 16552:16913008, 16913060 (1850)** — "the previously-mentioned **Akhúnd Mullá ‘Abdu'l-Husayn**,
  who had ascended the rooftop, was struck in the right foot during the first volley... **his was the first
  blood spilled on that land**"; the pious old man "running along and cheering."
- **Rabbani 16552:16913595, 16913654, 16949639 (NAYRÍZ-II, 1853)** — "**Mullá ‘Abdu'l-Husayn, the judge and
  soothsayer of Nayríz, who had stood with the illustrious Vahíd**" — captured at age eighty in 1853, "two
  of my sons were beheaded in front of my very own eyes," wife & other sons imprisoned; he **died en route
  to Shíráz and was decapitated.** His martyred sons: Mullá Muḥammad-Taqí and Mullá ‘Alí-Naqí
  (16552:16949618, 16949786).

**Chronology check:** the SAME man — wounded (first blood, "First Martyr of Nayriz" honorific) and SURVIVED
the 1850 Nayríz-I, then captured and MARTYRED with his family destroyed in the 1853 Nayríz-II. This is the
textbook **bridging-figure** pattern (cf. the skill's "Mírzá ‘Alí Sardár" example): explicit narrative
carry-forward = ONE person with a role-arc across two episodes, not two namesakes.

**RECOMMENDATION: ONE bridging figure.** Akhúnd / Mullá ‘Abdu'l-Ḥusayn of Nayríz (the judge/soothsayer,
the octogenarian who welcomed Vaḥíd) = DB's 1850 "man of eighty, first to suffer" = Ahdieh's man whose
family died in 1853. Capture as a single `Bábí` martyr entity with the arc: 1850 first-blood/wounded
("First Martyr of Nayríz" honorific, survived) → 1853 captured, sons beheaded before him, died/beheaded
en route to Shíráz. Link relationships: companion of Vaḥíd; father of martyrs Mullá Muḥammad-Taqí &
