# GPB Enrichment — Rulers & State Officials (Bahá'u'lláh → 'Abdu'l-Bahá periods)

> Verified vs GPB doc 21310 (¶=paragraph_index) + Balyuzi 462/466, Momen 11177. side per FINAL role; GPB framing flagged where it diverges from scholarship. Script aliases all low-confidence (English/translit corpus).

## OTTOMAN
### Sulṭán ‘Abdu'l-Majíd — opponent
- Reigned 1839–1861; recipient of *the Báb's* Tablet (¶21055517, w/ Najíb Páshá Válí of Baghdád). No epithet-characterization. trap: the Tablet was the BÁB's not Bahá'u'lláh's; Súriy-i-Mulúk targeted his successor. MERGE-TRAP: NOT the martyr Ḥájí ‘Abdu'l-Majíd (¶21055837, Badí‘'s father).

### Sulṭán ‘Abdu'l-‘Azíz — opponent
- "the despotic Sulṭán ‘Abdu'l-‘Azíz" (¶21055506); Aqdas "throne of tyranny" (¶21055900); "author of the calamities heaped upon Bahá'u'lláh… three decrees of banishment" (¶21055900). Reigned 1861–1876.
- Target of Súriy-i-Mulúk; fall foretold in Lawḥ-i-Fu'ád: deposed by palace revolution, condemned by fatvá, **assassinated 1876** (¶21055900). trap: distinguish from ‘Azíz Páshá (Adrianople asst-gov ¶21055761) + shrine ‘Abdu'l-‘Aẓím.

### Sulṭán ‘Abdu'l-Ḥamíd II — opponent
- "the 'Great Assassin'" + (GPB quoting external source) "the most mean, cunning, untrustworthy and cruel intriguer of the long dynasty of ‘Uthmán… twenty-three degenerate predecessors since… Sulaymán the Magnificent" (¶21055990). 'Abdu'l-Bahá's jailer; Commission of Inquiry. Deposed/deported 1909 (¶21055990). Reigned 1876–1909.
- trap: ‘Abdu'l-‘Azíz (exiled Bahá'u'lláh) vs ‘Abdu'l-Ḥamíd (jailed 'Abdu'l-Bahá) — never collapse. "23 predecessors" = GPB quoting another author, attribute correctly.

### ‘Alí Páshá (Grand Vizir / Ṣadr-i-A‘ẓam) — opponent
- denounced in **Lawḥ-i-Ra'ís**; "cruelty 'that hath made hell to blaze'" (¶21055858); fall predicted in Lawḥ-i-Fu'ád → "dismissed… shorn of all power… sank into complete oblivion" (¶21055916). With Fu'ád engineered the ‘Akká banishment. Name: Mehmed Emin ‘Álí Páshá.
- trap: Lawḥ-i-Ra'ís→‘Alí Páshá; Lawḥ-i-Fu'ád→about Fu'ád but predicts ‘Alí's fall — don't cross-wire. Distinguish from Ḥájí ‘Alí Khán Ḥájibu'd-Dawlih (Persian executioner).

### Fu'ád Páshá — opponent
- "the Turkish Minister for Foreign Affairs, denounced by Him as the 'instigator'" of the ‘Akká banishment; "struck down, while on a trip to Paris, by the avenging rod of God, and died at Nice (1869)" (¶21055916), as foretold in the **Lawḥ-i-Fu'ád**. trap: the Lawḥ-i-Fu'ád prophesies THREE falls (Fu'ád, ‘Alí Páshá, the Sulṭán).

## PERSIAN CROWN
### Náṣiri'd-Dín Sháh — opponent — ⚠ MERGED ENTITY (4 fragments)
- MERGE: "Náṣiri'd-Dín S̱háh" (sub-macron) + "Náṣiri'd-Dín Sháh" (plain) + "Náṣiri'd-Dín Mírzá" (pre-1848 crown-prince) + "the Qájár"/"two sovereigns" — ALL ONE (became Sháh 1848 age 17). Reigned 1848–1896.
- "the vain and despotic… 'Prince of Oppressors'… 'an object-lesson for the world'" (¶21055901); "capricious monarch… 'venal, artful and false' ministers" (¶21055832). Target of **Lawḥ-i-Sulṭán** ("lengthiest epistle to any single sovereign" ¶21055521). **Assassinated in the shrine of Sháh ‘Abdu'l-‘Aẓím on the eve of his jubilee (1896)** by Mírzá Riḍá, follower of Afghání (¶21055901/21056032) → eclipse of the Qájár dynasty.
- trap: as crown-prince presided over the Báb's 1848 Tabríz examination (same person). His father = Muḥammad Sháh (the other Qájár persecutor). Assassination first wrongly blamed on Bahá'ís → Varqá's martyrdom.

### Muẓaffari'd-Dín Sháh — opponent
- "the weak and timid Muẓaffar[i'd-Dín]" governor of Ádhirbáyján (¶21055832); "the Sháh's son and successor" under whom the Constitutional Movement entangled the persecuted community (¶21056032). Paris interview w/ 2 Bahá'í reps "no real results" (¶21056033). Reigned 1896–1907. Grand Vizir: Atábik-i-A‘ẓam.

### Reza Shah Pahlavi — opponent — ⚠ LIGHT / FLAG
- NOT in GPB main body (Formative-Age figure; GPB pub. 1944). Corpus confirms only accession 1925 / abdication 1941 / Qájár termination (Momen 11177 ¶11700418). The "banned Bahá'í literature/schools" claim = `likely` (community histories), did NOT verify cleanly → flag before stamping verified. trap: NOT son Mohammad Reza; "Riḍá" collides w/ Mírzá Riḍá (assassin) + ‘Alí-Riḍá (Báb's brother).

### Muḥammad Sháh / Ḥájí Mírzá Áqásí / Mírzá Taqí Khán (Amír-Niẓám) — ⚠ CONFIRM Báb-wave, DO NOT redo
- Báb-period figures, rich GPB material cited (Muḥammad Sháh "bigoted and irresolute" ¶21055600; Áqásí ¶21055506/544/581; Amír-Niẓám "obdurate, iron-hearted" ¶21055544, SKILL.md canonical case). Verify existing Báb-wave records carry the verbatim characterizations.

## PERSIAN MINISTERS
### Mírzá Áqá Khán-i-Núrí (I‘timádu'd-Dawlih) — opponent (qualified)
- Grand Vizir after Amír-Niẓám (1851–1858). ⚠ GPB NUANCE (capture): "had endeavored, at the very outset of his ministry, to effect a reconciliation between his government and the One Whom he regarded as the most resourceful of the Báb's disciples" (¶21055581). Nephew Mírzá Abú-Ṭálib Khán despoiled Bahá'u'lláh's Tákur home. trap: nisba "-i-Núrí" ties to Bahá'u'lláh's district but NOT a relative; don't paint uniformly villainous.

### Mírzá Ḥusayn Khán, the Mushíru'd-Dawlih — opponent — ⚠ MERGE 2 fragments
- Persian Ambassador at Constantinople; "denounced by Bahá'u'lláh as His 'calumniator'" (¶21055744); intrigued w/ ‘Alí & Fu'ád Páshá to procure the Adrianople banishment (¶21055788). Later Sipahsálár/Grand Vizir of Persia. MERGE the sub-macron + plain forms.
- ⚠ CRITICAL trap: do NOT merge with Ḥusayn Khán the Shíráz governor (next) — different title/period/role.

### Ḥusayn Khán Niẓámu'd-Dawlih (Ájúdán-Báshí) — opponent — ⚠⚠ THIS IS A BÁB-PERIOD FIGURE, route to Báb wave
- DECISIVE CORRECTION (cross-verified Balyuzi 466 ¶6715951 + 462 ¶5583960): "Ḥusayn Khán… called Ajudan-Bashi… titles Sahib-Ikhtiyar and Nizamu'd-Dawlih… native of Maraghih… envoy to London and Paris." = GPB's "Ḥusayn Khán, the governor of Shíráz, stigmatized as a 'wine-bibber' and a 'tyrant,' the first who arose to ill-treat the Báb" (¶21055601), condemned in the Báb's Tablet to Muḥammad Sháh (¶21055521). Governor of Fárs 1844–48. Fate: lost everything, died abandoned (¶21055601).
- ⚠ My brief mis-placed him as a Bahá'u'lláh-era minister. He is BÁB-PERIOD → confirm/route to Báb-wave record; do not create a duplicate. THREE "Ḥusayn Khán"s stay separate.

### Ẓillu's-Sulṭán (Prince Mas‘úd Mírzá) — opponent
- Náṣiri'd-Dín's eldest son (non-royal mother → barred from throne); "stigmatized by Bahá'u'lláh as 'the Infernal Tree'"; ruled >2/5 of the kingdom; orchestrated the Iṣfahán martyrdoms (King & Beloved of Martyrs); stripped of all governorships save Iṣfahán (¶21055916). Son: Jalálu'd-Dawlih (tyrant of Yazd). trap: father (Mas‘úd Mírzá) ≠ son Jalálu'd-Dawlih.

### Kámrán Mírzá (Náyibu's-Salṭanih) — opponent
- Náṣiri'd-Dín's son; Governor of Ṭihrán & War Minister; "sank into obscurity" (¶21056079); fled into hiding at the assassination (Balyuzi 3887 ¶7508965). trap: "Mírzá" = royal suffix (Prince Kámrán), not commoner prefix.

### Atábik-i-A‘ẓam (Mírzá ‘Alí-Aṣghar Khán) — opponent — MERGE w/ "Aminu's-Sulṭán"
- "the unprincipled and reactionary… Atábik-i-A‘ẓam"; Paris interview "no real results" (¶21056033); "sank into obscurity" (¶21056079). Grand Vizir under Náṣiri'd-Dín then Muẓaffari'd-Dín; also styled Aminu's-Sulṭán (Balyuzi). Assassinated 1907. MERGE the two titles → one person.

## EUROPEAN MONARCHS BAHÁ'U'LLÁH ADDRESSED (other — summoned sovereigns, not persecutors)
### Napoleon III — other
- "the foremost monarch of his day in the West, excessively ambitious, inordinately proud, tricky and superficial… contemptuously flung down the Tablet… tested by Him and found wanting" (¶21055902); "Chief of Sovereigns" who "'cast behind his back' the Tablet" (¶21055852). TWO Tablets (Adrianople + ‘Akká). Fall fulfilled: **Battle of Sedan 1870**, lost kingdom, son killed in Zulu War (¶21055902). Emperor 1852–1870.
- trap: NOT his uncle Napoleon I (besieger of ‘Akká, ¶21055804); GPB uses bare "Napoleon" for both — disambiguate by context.

### Queen Victoria — other — ⚠ UNIQUELY COMMENDED
- GPB records the Tablet's COMMENDATIONS: rewarded for "having 'forbidden the trading in slaves'… 'entrusted the reins of counsel into the hands of the representatives of the people'" (¶21055855); invited to "the Lesser Peace" (¶21055851); Parliament = "the Mother of Parliaments" (¶21055867). NO chastisement-fate. Recipient of Lawḥ-i-Malikih. Reigned 1837–1901; her granddaughter = Queen Marie of Rumania.
- trap: GPB's tone is commendatory — do NOT lump with the "fate of tyrants" monarchs. The favorable framing IS the point.

### Czar Alexander II — other
- "Nicolaevitch Alexander II, the all-powerful Czar of Russia… thrice warned… cautioned not to allow his sovereignty to prevent him from recognizing 'the Supreme Sovereign'" (¶21055905). Russia (via Prince Dolgorouki) had earlier helped free Bahá'u'lláh from the Síyáh-Chál. Fate: assassinated 1881 → revolution extinguished the Romanoffs (¶21055905). Recipient of Lawḥ-i-Malik-i-Rús. Emperor 1855–1881.
- trap: "Nicolaevitch" = patronymic (Alexander II Nikolaevich), NOT Nicholas. Distinguish from Alexander III + Nicholas II.

### Pope Pius IX — other
- Addressed by OFFICE: "the Sovereign Pontiff" (¶21055869). Recipient of **Lawḥ-i-Páp** (within Súriy-i-Haykal). Fate: "the virtual extinction of the temporal sovereignty of the Roman Pontiff" — loss of Papal States 1870 (¶21055907/911). Pope 1846–1878 (longest-reigning).
- trap: GPB never names "Pius IX" — binds to the office-holder at revelation. "Vicar of the Prophet of Islám" in the same sentence = the Ottoman Caliph (different office) — don't conflate.

### William I (Prussia/German Emperor) — other
- "the pride-intoxicated newly-acclaimed conqueror of Napoleon III, admonished in the Kitáb-i-Aqdas" (¶21055903); "the banks of the Rhine"/"lamentations of Berlin" prophecy (¶21055856). King of Prussia 1861, German Emperor 1871–1888. Fate threads through grandson William II (abdication, flight to Holland).
- trap: do NOT merge with grandson William II (same ¶) — the Aqdas address is to William I.

### Francis Joseph I of Austria — other
- "The arbitrary and unyielding Francis Joseph… reproved in the Kitáb-i-Aqdas, for having neglected his manifest duty to inquire about Bahá'u'lláh during his pilgrimage to the Holy Land" (¶21055904). Cascade of family tragedies (Maximilian, Rudolph, Empress Elisabeth, Franz Ferdinand) + empire's disintegration (¶21055904). Reigned 1848–1916.
- trap: chastisement = for NEGLECT (failing to inquire), not active persecution → other.

## BRITISH PALESTINE OFFICIALS, 'ABDU'L-BAHÁ ERA (other — PROTECTORS, GPB favorable)
### Lord Curzon — other — ⚠ primarily a SCHOLARLY WITNESS, not an administrator
- Most GPB hits = quoted authority on Bábí history (Ṭáhirih "one of the most affecting episodes in modern history" ¶21055589; "Tales of magnificent heroism" ¶21055598; "nineteen-twentieths" estimate ¶21055844; imparted "a vitality which no other impulse could have secured" ¶21055843). Protection role: Cabinet warned of the Haifa danger (¶21056054). Author of *Persia and the Persian Question* (1892). Viceroy of India 1899–1905; Foreign Secretary 1919–24.
- trap: do NOT class as persecutor or chiefly a Palestine official; he's GPB's most-cited English authority + a Cabinet-level protector.

### Sir Herbert Samuel — other — MERGE "Viscount Samuel of Carmel"
- First High Commissioner of Mandatory Palestine (1920–25); attended 'Abdu'l-Bahá's funeral 1921, "express my respect for His creed and my regard for His person" (¶21056067), walked behind the coffin. Churchill telegraphed condolences via him (¶21056065). MERGE w/ "Viscount Samuel of Carmel" (¶21056076).

### General Allenby (Viscount Allenby) — other
- On Balfour's instruction "extend every protection… to ‘Abdu'l-Bahá"; after taking Haifa cabled "notify the world that ‘Abdu'l-Bahá is safe," **frustrating the Turkish C-in-C's intention to "crucify ‘Abdu'l-Bahá and His family on Mt. Carmel"** (¶21056054). Later High Commissioner for Egypt. Conquest of Palestine 1918 lifted the 65-yr danger. trap: distinct from Generals Congreve + Money (¶21056065).

### Sir Ronald Storrs — other
- Governor of Jerusalem 1917–26; eulogized the funeral: "I have never known a more united expression of regret and respect…" (¶21056067/068). trap: distinct from Sir Stewart Symes (Governor of Phoenicia).

### Lord Lamington — other
- Met 'Abdu'l-Bahá in England; his Foreign Office letter "to 'explain the importance of ‘Abdu'l-Bahá's position'" triggered the Balfour→Allenby protection chain (¶21056054) — pivotal human link in the rescue. Baron Lamington (Charles Cochrane-Baillie), ex-Governor of Queensland & Bombay.
